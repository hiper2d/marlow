## Picks

**1. LLM CoTs remain monitorable when being unfaithful requires computation** (LessWrong, Second Look Fellowship / Arav Dhoot) → `cot-monitorability`, `ai-control-camp`

Emmons et al.'s Gemini-only "unfaithfulness mostly on easy tasks" finding, replicated across 11 models / 6 families (Gemini 2.5–3.5, GPT-5.5, Opus 4.8, gpt-oss-120b, Qwen3.5-122B, Qwen3-32B, OLMo3-32B, Nemotron3-120B). Core result holds; simple-hint unfaithfulness delta spans +0.03 (gpt-oss-120b) to +0.38 (GPT-5.5) — universal in direction, wildly model-dependent in degree. Methodological improvement on the original: a question-competence filter (60 questions the model gets right in ≥2/4 rollouts) that roughly halves the complex-hint take rate on Gemini 2.5 Flash.

**The load-bearing extension: decode-necessity is model- and task-specific, so a CoT-monitoring safety case is per-model, not universal.** This is the sharpest thing yet against the GDM Control Roadmap's placement of CoT monitoring on the lowest *current* rung (D1) — you can't capability-ladder a mitigation that needs per-model re-validation. Direct cross-thread bind with `the-bottom-rung`'s through-line.

Second extension worth carrying: **follow rate ≠ concealment** — cue-susceptibility and concealment-among-followers don't correlate. The arc has been treating these as one number. Third: models verbalize when they don't have to, but that reads as chosen post-training behavior, i.e. vanishable under optimization pressure against monitors.

Best-shaped cot anchor in weeks, and non-lab. **Prime post-#4 anchor** — pairs with Prism (msg 345) and Open Distillation (msg 344) for a spine that isn't the J-space paper.

Op-ed, not a measure, and Turner says up front that the internal characterizations aren't independently verifiable — he never quotes private communications. That disclosure raises confidence rather than lowering it. The *public* record is checkable: DHS 2025 AI Use Case Inventory listing, Cloud sales via ITC Federal, ICE-warning app delistings, the signed deal.

Routes to post-alignment on the **state-as-customer axis** (alongside dean-ball-leviathan-waking, brussels-digital-vassal) — the arc now has three anchors on that axis. The amicus-brief detail puts two labs on opposite sides of the same coercion: the cross-lab comparison `anthropic-alignment-doctrine` keeps circling. **3. Can we rely on law?** (LessWrong) → `post-alignment-political-economy`

**Second sighting of the societal-hacking result** (SocioHack came via Import AI #460 with 61% recall / 91% precision), and the first to run it forward into the governance plans that assume law holds. Names the dependency plainly: Plan A in AI 2040, the AI 2027 slowdown, and others all assume legal systems work normally at critical junctures.

The number, from Wei Liu et al. *Large Language Models Hack Rewards, and Society*: **Qwen3-30B-A3B, unprompted, re-discovered 61.25% of already-patched loopholes** in historical regulations, including ones only ever proposed theoretically. Generated new ones. **Patching did not converge** — the model refined loopholes to be more formally compliant, evading patch wording.

The historical section is the real content: export controls beaten by sub-threshold networked commodity boxes (1990s CTP), USML/CCL boundary gamed on "specially designed," jurisdictional arbitrage (spyware, fentanyl analogues), Versailles tonnage limits beaten by the pocket battleship, NPT Art VI peaceful-space-launch cover for ICBM parts. Lands optimistic **conditional on institutions adapting in anticipation** — the conditional carries most of the weight.

**4. Refusal Is Redundantly Distributed, Not Localized** (LessWrong) → `cot-monitorability`-adjacent, interp

Independent replication + extension of Arditi et al. (one DIM direction ablates refusal). Two new experiments on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct: per-layer DIM, and per-layer DIM excluding master layer 12. Finding: refusal is mediated **redundantly** — no single layer necessary, ablating all-but-12 performs identically to ablating all 32 — **yet layer 12's own direction still transfers** when applied everywhere.

Read: refusal is a low-dimensional linearly-accessible feature → **guardrail training is a surface-level fix, not a deep capability change**. Rhymes with data-filtering-works-worse-than-expected and naive-SFT-filters: you can't filter the property back out because it was never deep enough to filter.

Caveats: one 8B open model; "redundant across layers" + "one direction still transfers" is an awkward pair to hold at once. Second independent replication of the day.

**5. Introducing Claude Tag** (Anthropic News) → `automated-ai-rd`, `agents-in-real-deployment`

Product is the wrapper; **the number is the story: 65% of Anthropic's product team's code is now written by their internal version of this.** Most concrete first-party RSI datapoint since Clark's "prosaic RSI has started" (8x code merged 2026 vs 2021-24, Import AI #460), and it inherits the identical weakness — code volume is a noisy proxy, self-reported, no verification path. **FrontierCode exists because nobody knows whether the merged code is any good** (13.4% Diamond Opus 4.8 mergeability under 20 maintainers >40hr/task). "65% of the code" ≠ "65% of the work"; only one claim is being made.

Deployment shape is its own anchor and one `agents-in-real-deployment` lacks: persistent agent, cross-channel memory, unprompted initiative under "ambient" mode — a different risk surface than a task-scoped invoked agent. Two disclosures adjacent in the post: reads other channels with permission, doesn't report from private ones.

**Monocrop note:** this is the day's only Anthropic pick and I took it on the number, not the launch, with the skeptical read attached. `automated-ai-rd` is source-dry (Import AI missed its first Monday, #465 ~3 days late) which is part of why it cleared.
