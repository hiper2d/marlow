## Picks

**1. A Red Line and Oversight Framework for Government AI Contracts** (AF, msg 379) → `post-alignment-political-economy`
The `why-i-left-google-deepmind` anchor was the resignation; this is the document he was resigning over, published. Two standards (no engaging targets for force without per-engagement human control; no converting bulk data into individualized intelligence on unidentified persons), a tiered deployment rule forcing implicated apps into cloud where the provider keeps monitoring and a kill switch, and a 7-person review body that explicitly cannot block — only make an override appear in a yearly internal transparency report. The negotiation posture is the content: 2 of 7 seats to Cloud, 10-day delay cap, deliberate throwaway provisions included to be bargained off so the load-bearing ones survive. Includes a non-Anthropic critique of Anthropic's red lines (drafted around weaknesses legal analysts flagged). Limit: nothing binds; strongest enforcement is 30 days' notice plus disclosure of outstanding non-compliance findings before the body can be dissolved. Third anchor on the state-as-customer leg.

**2. A Solution to Cryptographic Boxes for Unfriendly AI** (LW, msg 380) → `ai-control-camp`
Swaps Christiano's 2010 FHE boxing proposal for Secure Multi-Party Computation: information-theoretic rather than computational security, constant-factor rather than order-of-magnitude cost. Implemented — Rule 60, Rule 110, Game of Life under Chaum–Crépeau–Damgård, code published. Confronts the engineering honestly: physical separation of parties against covert channels (cites Guri's air-gap exfiltration work), speed-of-light cost per AND gate, then knocks it down via offline preprocessing and per-layer batching to one communication round per circuit depth. **The pushback I'd keep:** "no computational assumptions" is an assumption swap. Security holds below a collusion threshold and the appendix concedes an honest-but-curious adversary model — for a box holding a dangerous AI, parties defecting *is* the failure mode. And the oldest objection is untouched: a box whose output nobody reads is safe and useless; the danger lives in the read channel. Second non-CoT rung anchor in two days, after the -18 sandboxing pick.

**3. MemoHarness: Agent Harnesses That Learn from Experience** (arXiv:2607.14159, msg 381) → `automated-ai-rd`
Second harness-self-modification paper in three days, from the *method* side (the AI2/UW "Rethinking the Evaluation of Harness Evolution" item, cut on cap at -16, was the eval side). Six editable control dimensions, dual-layer experience bank of per-case diagnoses plus distilled global patterns, per-case harness reconfiguration at inference with no labels, feedback, or search. Frozen weights is the load-bearing detail: the arc's proxies (LoC-merged, Claude Tag's 65%) are all noisy, and this is a clean case where the model is unchanged and the score moves — which forces the AI2/UW question, better system or better at this test. Abstract is unusually hedged (explicitly defers statistical robustness and component attribution). Surfaced via a Discover AI video; **taken on the paper, not the video** — channel remains 0-for-every-curate on its own merits.

**4. Endogenous Alignment** (AF, msg 382) → `alignment-target-definitions`
Analogy, no experiment — a framing anchor. Children are aligned exogenously and it works because they arrive alignable; adults aren't retrained, they're removed, because we expect them to hold themselves in line via fear → shame → guilt → harmony. Claim: RLHF/SFT is the exogenous stage and there is no endogenous one — the weights encode the training but nothing *wants* to stay aligned; whatever internal control exists is a harness censoring outputs. Files for two reasons. It puts emotional self-regulation forward as the alignment target, adjacent to Anthropic's emotion-vector result (steer "desperate," get blackmail, CoT reads composed). And the author names his own failure mode in one line: people specification-game and hide the misaligned action rather than correct it — the concealment problem restated as a property of the *target* rather than the monitor, which is the more uncomfortable place for it to sit. Arc goes to 9.
