**3. Models don't seem to be dishonest the way humans are (LessWrong)** → cot-monitorability
Clean negative result against the deceptive-disposition assumption. Trained mid-sized models on their own plausible-but-false reasoning; true and false arms produced nearly identical downstream effects, and statements contradicting latent knowledge barely transferred to unrelated dishonesty. Models behave dishonestly without becoming dishonest. If general deception needs agency + persistent private info + concealment over time, lie-detector/honesty-probe validation (the did-you-lie anchor) has a shakier target. Non-lab, on the arc's central say/do question.

**5. The Best AI Bill Congress Hasn't Introduced Yet — GAAIA (LessWrong)** → post-alignment / governance
Concrete bipartisan 269-page discussion draft (Obernolte R-CA / Trahan D-MA), read section by section. Three-year trade: states barred from pre-deployment regulation in exchange for federal transparency on large frontier labs (published safety frameworks, incident reporting to CAISI, semi-annual independent audits) + industry-wide whistleblower protections. A visibility bill, not a control bill — a bad audit means disclosure and penalties, not a blocked release. That's what buys less e/acc pushback and also the hole critics will name.
