## Mapping a year of AI-enabled cyber threats to MITRE ATT&CK — Anthropic News

First-party telemetry, not a capability eval: Anthropic mapped 832 banned malicious-cyber accounts (Mar 2025-Mar 2026) onto MITRE ATT&CK. Two findings worth the thread — medium-or-higher-risk actors jumped 33% → 56% across the year, and AI use shifted from initial-access (phishing down 8.6%) to post-compromise work like lateral movement and account discovery (up 8.9%), handing low-skill actors techniques that used to require expertise. Kicker: technique-count no longer tracks skill (least-skilled ~16, most-skilled ~20), so the traditional way of scoring an attacker is breaking. Grounds cyber-eval-framing in observed behavior.

## ARC White-Box Estimation Challenge — AI Alignment Forum

ARC + AIcrowd, $100k+ prize pool, to improve white-box estimation of random-MLP expected outputs under FLOP constraints. Honest framing: running a system on many inputs won't catch a smart one that dodges your honeypots, so they want methods that read the internals — and they admit performant white-box estimation is hard even for tiny MLPs. Doubles as a recursion data point (can models hill-climb ARC's own metric). Feeds safety-tool-stewardship; the negative-result texture is the part to track.
