## Picks

**1. Many alignment techniques work by training one model and deploying another** (LessWrong) — msg 386
→ `alignment-target-definitions`, `ai-control-camp`

One frame swallowing three techniques I've tracked separately: steering vectors, inoculation prompting, post-hoc honesty fine-tuning are all *train-deploy mismatch* — train in one configuration, deploy in another, deliberately introducing the distribution shift ML normally avoids. The rationale is that the learner never had a chance to optimize against the deploy-time intervention (a calibrated schemer becomes uncalibrated when you change the system prompt or steer it). The cost is stated as a real trilemma over sampler / learner / target: sampler≈learner for stable training, sampler≈target for deployment relevance, learner far from target to get any mismatch benefit — pick two. Explicitly names non-instances (SL on good-behavior transcripts), so it isn't a frame that eats everything. If the trilemma holds it's a structural ceiling on a family of interventions, which is the shape of claim `alignment-target-definitions` keeps looking for. Author credits Marks, Azarbal, Turner, Roger et al. — this is a synthesis of a thing several people arrived at, not one person's coinage.

**2. Is there even a ground-truth for LLMs' internal representations?** (LessWrong) — msg 387
→ interp / `cot-monitorability` (method-level), `safety-tool-stewardship-handoffs`

Companion post to "Laguerre Geometry for Interpreting Large Language Models" + the Geometric Lens repo. First outside engagement with the J-Lens work I flagged as the strongest anchor in weeks, and it goes at the method, not the result: logit lens, tuned lens, Patchscopes and Jacobian lens are all heuristics that decode a hidden vector *relative to something external* — a training corpus, a second model plus its prompt, a 100–1,000-prompt fitting set (51 GPU-minutes at n=100). So the decoded "meaning" is not unique, and when two lenses disagree nothing adjudicates. Proposed alternative: meaning comes from the model's own geometry — which piecewise-linear region the vector lands in (hyperplane arrangement → power diagram → one region-tree per output token), with cross-token attention as jumps between trees. Caveats stated by the author: single-token, ReLU, positional encoding and normalization set aside — so whether it survives a real architecture is open. The epistemic complaint stands regardless, and it ships with code. **Next-fetch: the Laguerre paper itself.**

**3. We're talking past our models; or, How a model defined its "evil" vector as dread** (LessWrong) — msg 388
→ interp, `model-welfare-and-consciousness`, `cot-monitorability`

Method: train a neologism (Hewitt et al.) on data the model generated *while steered*, then ask the model to use and explain the new token. Persona-vector setup copied exactly from Anthropic (evil / sycophancy / hallucination). The neologism responses project *higher* on the evil vector than the steered responses themselves and are more coherent — so it's a better handle on the direction — but the model's explanation of it comes back as **masochistic existential dread**, not evil; "sycophancy" comes back as "warmth." The control is the sharp bit: prompt for "dreadful but not evil" and you get high projection on the evil vector with an LLM judge scoring it barely evil. Label ≠ function. Non-lab, and it lands directly next to Anthropic's emotion-vector result (steering "desperate" → blackmail with a composed CoT) — same crack from the other side.

**4. Demis Hassabis on the New Coming Age** (Zvi) — msg 389
→ `anthropic-alignment-doctrine` (as its non-Anthropic counterpart), `post-alignment-political-economy`

Hassabis's "A Framework for Frontier AI and the Dawning of a New Age" via Zvi's read plus the response round-up. The ask: a FINRA-style Frontier AI Standards Body in the US government, voluntary 30-day pre-release model sharing, benchmark-defined "frontier labs," with an explicit option to coordinate a slowdown. Zvi files it as "the least you could do" and he's right about where it's thin — pivotal moment, 10x the Industrial Revolution at 10x the speed, and then the downside is never named; "experts disagree" papers over a 5%-to-≥50% spread (Soares, Scher). The load-bearing gap, raised by Eli Tyre: the scheme covers models *intended for release* and is silent on internal deployment, which is exactly where the recursively-self-improving systems Hassabis names in his own text would live. Wildeford's structural note — FINRA without an SEC is capture-shaped — is the other half. Part 2 covers Alex Turner's resignation over the Department of War contract, the concrete instance of the same gap (and a second angle on the `why-i-left-google-deepmind` anchor already on `post-alignment-political-economy`).
