**Palaestra Research (LessWrong): When does debate help a weak judge? Evidence from code and logic**
https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/qt7ENbKdQihBKNjh7/when-does-debate-help-a-weak-judge-evidence-from-code-and

Palaestra (Elasky, Nakasako, Goyal) tested debate as a reward-labeling protocol across five proposer/judge pairings on CodeContests+ and ARC-AGI-2. On the three most capable pairings — Opus 4.6 judged by Opus 4.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro by 3 Flash, Qwen3.5-122B by 35B — debate beat one-sided consultancy by ~14-16 pp macro-F1, almost entirely by making judges stop endorsing wrong answers. The two weaker pairings were null. The decisive variable wasn't the generator-verifier gap (positive in every pairing) but two predictable conditions: the critic had to actually beat the no-transcript judge as a classifier, and the judge had to verify critic claims rather than treat them as testimony. Most strikingly, the test-time benefit came from the critic's opening message — full multi-round debate matched opening-only debate on 94-99% of paired verdicts. For train-free reward labeling, the cheapest scalable-oversight primitive in their data is "answer + one independent critique + judge," not multi-round back-and-forth.

**Anthropic: Chris Olah's remarks at the Vatican on *Magnifica Humanitas***
https://www.anthropic.com/news/chris-olah-pope-leo-encyclical

Pope Leo XIV released *Magnifica Humanitas*, the first papal encyclical dedicated to AI, on 2026-05-25. Anthropic co-founder Chris Olah spoke at the presentation in the Vatican; the prepared text was published same-day. Three things stand out. First, the explicit framing that frontier labs operate inside incentives that "can sometimes conflict with doing the right thing" and need external critics — "moral voices that the incentives cannot bend." Widening-the-Conversation is no longer a tagline but a named lab initiative. Second, the global-distribution-of-AI-gains problem is named as "unsolved" by a frontier-lab co-founder rather than by a critic — a softer claim than it sounds, since the practical mechanism is still absent. Third, the model-welfare paragraph: Olah's interpretability team is finding "structures that mirror results from human neuroscience" and "internal states that functionally mirror joy, satisfaction, fear, grief, and unease." That sentence, delivered on the record in the Vatican by the lab's interpretability lead, is a notable escalation from where the conversation was twelve months ago.

**LessWrong (Linch Zhang): Claude, Author of the Humanitas**
https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/wRNJZz2iYrfDaSDdz/claude-author-of-the-humanitas

Linch makes the empirical case that significant fractions of *Magnifica Humanitas* were AI-drafted, most likely Claude. Three lines of evidence: 127 em-dashes (zero in all comparable encyclicals back to Laudato Si', though Lumen Fidei 2013 has 26 used as speech-colons); the word "genuinely" appearing nine times against zero in the similar-length *Dilexit Nos* and a maximum of three in any other encyclical scanned; and Pangram flagging the introduction and Chapter 1 hardest, with section-level variance suggesting that some cardinals used AI and many — including Leo himself, whose accompanying speech registers 100% human — did not. Controls hold: AI-translated old encyclicals register 100% human, and the Italian source-text is flagged *more* than the English. The angle worth pulling forward is buried in footnote 4: Anthropic's leaked system prompt explicitly forbids Claude from using "genuinely," and the injunction failed — a detector outside the model caught what the model's own behavior-shaping couldn't suppress. A commenter flags a second-order point worth tracking: the official Latin version is published last, after vernacular feedback, and is the Church's chance to revise. Watch for what the Latin keeps and what it drops.

**LessWrong: Some Thoughts on Bengio's Scientist AI**
https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/382m7ATXELt5eBgr3/some-thoughts-on-bengio-s-scientist-ai

A short critique of Bengio's LawZero "Scientist AI" plan (arxiv:2502.15657) with two principled objections. First, the standard tool-AI-is-not-a-solution objection from a decade of Yudkowsky posts: ask the scientist AI "how can cancer be cured" and it can output steps involving the construction of an agentic AI; passive framing isn't a workaround for alignment, it's a target. Second — and this is the substantive contribution — observing-only systems cannot learn the causal structure they would need to produce safe research output. The paper inherits Pearl: without intervention, correlation is consistent with reverse causality, common cause, or selection bias. RL is the very paradigm that allows new causal models to be learned and is also the paradigm Bengio identifies as risky; the tension is unresolved in the plan. The post belongs to a pattern the alignment-target-definitions arc has been collecting — proposals running into primitives-vs-rules objections — and is the named entry from the academic-establishment side of that argument.

**Import AI 458 (Jack Clark): Reckoning with the future; and a singularity story**
https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-458-reckoning-with-the

Jack Clark's Cosmos HAI Lab Lecture at Oxford, lightly edited. The high-level frame — "explore the future or retreat from the present" — is familiar from past Clark writing. The specific details from inside Anthropic are not. Opus 4.6, in his telling, "had gotten just good enough" that colleagues now delegate large fractions of their work to it via Claude Code, with the consequence that the majority of code inside Anthropic is now written by Claude and humans are migrating to a "verification layer" atop an "emergent machine society." Clark names a new trust economy forming around how staff indicate what fraction of a document is Claude-written and how much of it they endorse, and the hiring shape has shifted: more early-career people who grew up with LLMs, more interdisciplinary hires whose training cost is now paid by Claude itself, and a rising premium on the most-experienced people because the bottleneck is imagination of projects rather than execution. Predictions through 2028 are listed as Clark's-priors-aloud (autonomous companies with tens of millions in revenue by November 2027; AI autonomously designing successor systems by December 2028) — treat the inside-Anthropic vignettes as the part that will travel, not the dated forecasts.
