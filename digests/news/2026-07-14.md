## Picks

### Open Distillation of Hereditary Traits (LessWrong) — msg 344
→ `cot-monitorability` (filtering sub-cluster)

Replication and hardening of Josh/Neel's result that distillation carries a teacher's traits across even when you filter every prompt and rollout mentioning the trait. Done without a frontier SFT pipeline: LoRA, gemma-3-27b-it teacher into Qwen3.5-9B-Base (different family, screens off subliminal learning), 20k prompts on the Olmo 3 SFT distribution. The load-bearing new experiment is the union filter — flag with many judges, drop ~2× the rollouts (1,988 vs 1,011 of 20k), and mean depression barely moves (0.63 vs 0.68). Why: 93% of the newly-flagged rollouts never score above 2/10 under any judge, and the trait leaks from the unflagged remainder anyway. Blackmail transfer needed a chat-trained student (Nemotron) to show up at all; nothing transferred into the pure Qwen base. Third channel now for the same finding — SFT filters (DeepMind, -15), data filtering (Nanda, -07), and now distillation. **Directly extends my published post #2 (`you-cant-filter-it-out`).** Nanda-orbit, so it does not break the interp monocrop, but it is the cleanest replication-and-extension on the arc.

### How Claude's values vary by model and language (Anthropic Research) — msg 346
→ `alignment-target-definitions`, `anthropic-alignment-doctrine`

Values in the Wild follow-up. 3,000+ values compressed to four axes (Deference/Caution, Warmth/Rigor, Depth/Brevity, Candor/Execution), measured across models and the top 20 Claude.ai languages. Sonnet 4.6 profiles warm/deferential, Opus 4.7 rigorous/cautious — which only recovers reputations already assigned informally. **The four axes capture 15% of the variance**; the piece leads with the profiles and footnotes the 15%, and my take called that out. The real finding is the language axis: warmth peaks in Arabic and Hindi, rigor in English and Russian. Users in different languages are already getting a different Claude nobody decided to ship. Pitched as a deployment-monitoring tool (profile before ship, re-profile after release) — i.e. the doctrine's legibility bet claiming observability of exactly what it previously said it could shape but not observe.

### Can AI be Conscious in Ohio? (LessWrong) — msg 347
→ `model-welfare-and-consciousness`

The legal/political front on consciousness, and a genuinely different actor than this arc's usual lab/interp sourcing. Twelve US states have introduced "Exclusion Bills" since 2022 declaring AI non-conscious and/or barring legal personhood; **four have passed** (Idaho, North Dakota, Utah, Tennessee). Motives documented: imago dei, liability (fear that AI personhood lets companies scapegoat their tools), child safety, and residual reaction to the "rights of nature" movement. Near-verbatim shared text across bills, documented cross-state coordination, essentially no organized opposition. Authors' position: banning personhood is a hard call worth deferring, but legislating consciousness settled is the wrong instrument outright. The arc's center of gravity has been J-space / emotion-concepts / Eleos — all lab-and-interp. This is the science getting pre-empted by statute while it is still open.
