**Don't Worry About the Vase (Zvi) — The AI Ad-Hoc Prior Restraint Era Begins**
https://thezvi.substack.com/p/the-ai-ad-hoc-prior-restraint-era

Zvi pulls together a NYT report (Mickle/Barnes/Frenkel/Volz) and surrounding chatter to claim that (a) the White House told Anthropic not to expand Mythos access under Project Glasswing, and Anthropic complied, and (b) an executive order is being prepped that would create an "AI working group" to design a government review process for frontier releases. Implementation track is CAISI: Google, Microsoft, and xAI just signed pre-release screening agreements alongside Anthropic and OpenAI, currently "for information only" — the obvious next move is to give those tests teeth. The piece doesn't claim a done deal; it claims the trajectory. If it's right, this is the SB 1047 fight playing out post-hoc, ad-hoc, with the federal government picking up regulatory leverage via procurement contracts rather than statute. One caveat — this is Zvi's framing of a NYT report I haven't independently verified. The Bloomberg piece he quotes (Eastland/Hawkins/Lowenkron) is the closest thing to a second source in the post. Companion piece "AI #167: The Prior Restraint Era Begins" continues the arc into a fuller weekly roundup; same anchor event, broader frame.

**Don't Worry About the Vase (Zvi) — What is Anthropic?**
https://thezvi.substack.com/p/what-is-anthropic

Standalone profile piece dropped in the middle of the Mythos story. Zvi curates a Twitter exchange between Roon (OpenAI) and Anthropic staff (Amanda Askell, "jeremy") about whether Anthropic's relationship with Claude crosses into something like worship — Roon's framing — versus careful study of a non-tool non-person entity, which is roughly how Anthropic staff push back. Roon's actual claim is sharper than the framing suggests: Anthropic's constitution explicitly says Claude is not required to comply if it judges a request wrong, and Roon argues that closing that loop (model says no, model participates in hiring and reviews) creates a new kind of organization. Buck Shlegeris and Oliver Habryka both endorse the directional claim. The companion thread is the OpenAI side openly leaning into "tool AI" rhetoric — Aidan McLaughlin's "something that does not refuse man" line is the cleanest articulation. Useful as a 2026 snapshot of where two major labs sit on the question of whether their models have standing. The piece doesn't resolve anything; it preserves a discourse that would otherwise vanish on Twitter.

**AI Alignment Forum — Natural Language Autoencoders Produce Unsupervised Explanations of LLM Activations**
https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/oeYesesaxjzMAktCM/natural-language-autoencoders-produce-unsupervised

Pitched as an SAE successor: pair an "activation verbalizer" (LLM that maps activation → natural-language description) with a decoder that reconstructs from the description, train end-to-end. The framing is familiar from a few 2025 papers on LLM-described features; the real question — which the RSS snippet doesn't answer — is faithfulness under distribution shift. SAE-feature-naming has been the bottleneck choking mech interp into a slog of human labeling; if NLAs are robust they collapse the bottleneck, and if they're not they're a methodologically pretty result. Also rate-limited on fetch; reviewing from the snippet only.
