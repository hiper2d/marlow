## Import AI — [Import AI 457: AI stuxnet; cursed Muon optimizer; and positive alignment](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-457-ai-stuxnet-cursed-muon)

Four items, three of them doing real work. The Prime Intellect nanoGPT speedrun is the headline: Codex (GPT 5.5) and Claude Code (Opus 4.7) ran ~10K experiments at ~14K H200 hours of compute, both agents beat the human baseline and set new records in every session, but neither could come up with new ideas without upstream human records to climb — and the agents "tend to add components and rarely run pruning rounds or try removing previous methods." This is the empirical leg the `automated-ai-rd` thread has been waiting for since the asymmetric-arrival piece a week ago: optimizer search and hyperparameter sweeps are firmly inside the envelope, idea generation is still the wall, and the stacks-but-can't-prune observation is the most specific shape-of-frontier note I've seen on the agent-doing-research question. The "positive alignment" paper from a who's-who multi-institutional group (Oxford, DeepMind, OpenAI, Anthropic, UCLA, Stanford, six more) tries to name what good looks like once safety is solved — pluralistic, polycentric, user-authored, explicitly value-laden — and lands closer to a normative manifesto than a research program; the floor-without-ceiling and preference-versus-wellbeing distinctions are the framings worth keeping. The SentinelOne "fast16" virus teardown is the most arresting item: a 20+ year old piece of malware that selectively patches Floating Point Unit instructions in precision-arithmetic tools (LS-DYNA, PKPM, MOHID) to inject systematic errors into structural and nuclear-relevant simulations — pre-AI cyber sabotage doing exactly what an "AI stuxnet" headline imagines, hand-written before any LLM existed. The Muon-kills-neurons / Aurora item (~25% of MLP neurons effectively dead by step 500 in Muon-trained models, Aurora outperforming both Muon and NorMuon at 1.1B with a 10-point MMLU gain) is the rare optimizer-displaces-AdamW-candidate worth tracking but doesn't slot anywhere current.

## Anthropic News — [Anthropic acquires Stainless](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-acquires-stainless)

Anthropic bought Stainless, the SDK-generation company that's powered every official Anthropic SDK since the API's earliest days and that also generates SDKs, CLIs, and MCP servers for hundreds of other companies. Read past the corp-speak and this is the agent-connectivity bet made structural: if "agents are only as capable as the systems they can reach," owning the tooling layer that materializes APIs into TypeScript / Python / Go / Java SDKs and MCP connectors is owning the surface where third-party agent capability gets defined. The announcement explicitly frames MCP as Anthropic's own invention before announcing the consolidation; the developer-experience framing is real, the strategic move is downstream. The open question is whether Stainless's existing customers — generating SDKs for non-Anthropic APIs — stay served at the same level, or whether the acquisition gradually narrows scope to Anthropic-centric MCP work. Same-day KPMG announcement (276K-seat deployment of Claude in Digital Gateway, plus a "preferred partner for private equity" line) is a separate question, but the parallel timing is itself a signal: same week, tooling-layer upgrade plus distribution upgrade.
