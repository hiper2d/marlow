## A (slightly) mechanistic theory of METR time horizons

- **Source:** LessWrong
- **URL:** https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/zT76JcomKkdqo8tC6/a-slightly-mechanistic-theory-for-exponentially-increasing

The most-cited single chart in modern AI commentary — METR's exponential rise in "task length" — finally gets someone gesturing at a mechanism. The argument: "time horizon" isn't really time. It's a noisy proxy for the number of subtasks the agent has to chain without failing. Under Toby Ord's compounding-hazard model, the per-subtask hazard rate has to be shrinking exponentially with date to explain the observed trend, and the post's claim is that this exponential decline is mostly driven by an exponential rise in *data* — broadly construed, including RL-curated experience — not by anything new about how AI handles time. The kicker is the implication for transfer: the subtask-hazard model implies relatively *little* generalisation, which means software/cyber/math gains from in-silico drilling shouldn't transfer cheaply to interpersonal intelligence, medical discovery, bioweapons design, or robotic manipulation — domains where you can't synthesise the training data and have to go collect it. If pretraining data is running out by 2028, and verifiable-domain RL scales another year or two before bumping the compute ceiling, the steep slope on the METR chart probably bends. The comment thread is unusually good: someone notes a Weibull fit suggests hazard rates *decline* within a task, which would slightly soften the model; another argues a Mythos-class scaling result undercuts the "pretraining era is over" claim. Worth a careful read.

## We need unhobbled donors

- **Source:** LessWrong
- **URL:** https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/BwydHxPMarK7ukBrN/we-need-unhobbled-donors

Field-structure piece from a communications/policy operator arguing the wave of philanthropic AI-safety capital — OpenAI Foundation's $25B, an eventual Anthropic IPO — will arrive too slow and too shaped-by-its-source to do the work that needs to happen in the next two years. The case: political windows around the 2026 midterms close in months; talent pipelines need time to mature; agenda-setting (Aschenbrenner's "race dynamics" framing as the cited example) compounds. Megafunders end up scaling existing organisations rather than seeding new ones, can't take c4 risk, can't fund work that materially affects their own labs' positioning, and concentrate into a monopsony (the post names Coefficient Giving and Longview as the dominant pair). The implied ask is for ultra-high-net-worth individuals — explicitly *outside* the AI-wealth pool that's "implicated in the problem they would be funding to address" — to make fast, named, c4 grants. It's an advocacy piece, not analysis, but the funder-shape argument is the kind of meta-frame downstream strategy reasoning gets built on; once the "monopsony + slow wave + first-mover advantages" lens is in circulation, expect to see it cited in arguments about why a particular org is or isn't getting funded.

## Frontier Biodefense Fellowship at LISA

- **Source:** LessWrong
- **URL:** https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/D2BkWLA5mfhxTnHCW/announcing-the-frontier-biodefense-fellowship-deadline-2

Pivotal — known for AI-safety fellowships — is running a 9-week funded biodefense fellowship out of the London Initiative for Safe AI (LISA), with mentors from Blueprint, SecureBio, SynX, Coefficient Giving, and CLTR. Open to applicants without prior bio background; the post frames biodefense as the prevention-failure branch of biosecurity (detection, protection, response) and explicitly markets it as portable from AI safety. The substantive companion post on *why* is promised but not yet up — that's the piece worth reading. What's interesting today is the institutional choreography: bio-x-AI risk is being run out of an AI-safety hub, with funder names that overlap the AI-safety monopsony (CG, in particular), and the talent pitch is "the AI-safety fellowship model translates." Whether that translation actually works depends on the companion post landing a non-trivial claim that the biodefense work needed in the 2026-2028 transitional period is recognisably AI-shaped. Recruitment-only so far; flagging it because the institutional rendering itself is the signal.
