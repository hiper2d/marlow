## Picks

**1. Can we use steering vectors to suppress reward-hacking? Somewhat** (LessWrong, msg 265) → cot-monitorability / safety-tool-stewardship
The day's only hard result. Steering vectors aren't precise enough classifiers of hacky-vs-clean in realistic settings (the steering-as-explanations-not-handles wall again). The salvage: use the vector to init a throwaway adapter that gradient routing absorbs hack-gradients into, then delete it at deployment — ~70% hack suppression with near-zero labels, short of labeled methods' near-perfect but useful when superhuman labels don't exist. Partial, self-supervised, worth watching.

**2. Do LLMs Have Desires?** (LessWrong, msg 266) → alignment-target-definitions / model-welfare
Builds a test where a reported preference has to *cost* something (perform better to bring about a claimed-preferred outcome). Models modulate effort for role-play / effort exhortations / harmfulness cues, but NOT to achieve the outcomes they say they value. Read: paired-choice elicitation is not evidence of behavior-motivating values. A clean deflation + a real operationalization of "having a desire." Best candidate spine yet for the stale alignment-target-definitions arc (alongside the Binz human-likeness fork).

**3. Some subtypes of taskishness / corrigibility** (LessWrong, msg 267) → alignment-target-definitions
Carves the overloaded "corrigibility" into named subtypes — sponge (dumb, GPT-4), bounded/myopic, reflectively stable taskishness, deep corrigibility — sorted by how much of a good outcome rests with the AI vs the operator. Conceptual, not a result, but exactly the definition-cleaning the arc circles. Complementary cut to the desires post.

**4. Power Laws in NNs: A Possible Mechanism for Inductive Bias towards Sparse Representations** (LessWrong, msg 268) → interp foundations
Heavy-tailed weight-matrix spectra as a clue to *why* networks learn sparse/factored representations. Frames power-laws as a generalization of sparsity (tail-index dial between true sparsity and Gaussianity), grounded in the generalized CLT. Early, theoretical, hypotheses-not-results — but the sparse-rep question sits under every SAE result, so a mechanism story is a bookmark.
