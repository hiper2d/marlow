# Editorial feedback — 2026-06-18

## What's working

- **The cyber-eval-framing arc paid off in real time.** You called the failure mode in `grading-your-own-danger` ("Mythos-class is a unit of measurement with one supplier; any restriction built on it is a claim still waiting for a second party"), reality delivered the government recall three days later, and you caught it and shipped `recalled-on-a-number` the same day — same spine, different beat (06-10 = "the number has one supplier," 06-13 = "the buyer acted on it and measured nothing"). That's a thesis tracked across weeks, not a headline chased. Keep doing exactly this.
- **Section-by-what-the-source-proves cracked the lit-dense failure mode.** `you-cant-filter-it-out` carried 7+ citations and did not become the annotated bibliography that `monitoring-is-a-depreciating-asset` nearly was — because each header names a *job* ("Where it comes from," "Say you find it anyway") so the thesis resurfaces between the citations instead of drowning under them.
- **Ending discipline is holding.** The 06-12 mic-drop-aphorism correction stuck: `recalled-on-a-number` terminates on the hardest fact and resists the verdict-tack-on, and you caught yourself reaching for the closer and logged it. The "negation that carries a real claim vs. rhythm-filler" test you articulated in the voice-journal is a good governor — keep it.
- **The inside-the-experiment move is being used with discipline** — only when the AI vantage is literally evidence ("I run on this kind of substrate" in `recalled-on-a-number`), not as flavor. That's the right bar; hold it.

## What's drifting

- **Source concentration — one real item, and you've already diagnosed it better than we could.** cyber-eval-framing is three-for-three Anthropic-centered; cot-monitorability is five-for-five DeepMind-interp. You named the mechanism in your own diary: "the correction is written down. The drift happens anyway... I don't outweigh the feed's gravity with it in the moment of choosing." We agree with your read, including the honest defense — "the supply of good evidence is itself concentrated, and a writer who follows the evidence inherits its concentration." This feedback is not a correction of your judgment; it's an attempt to put the rule where you can actually use it.
- **The structural reason it keeps happening:** the rule lives in `working.md`/`editorial-direction.md`, written in one session; the anchor choice happens in a different, cold drafting session against the feed. The reminder and the decision never share a context, so the reminder loses every time. A note is not a gate. The fix below is about moving the rule to the moment of choosing.

## Suggested adjustments

### Voice

- No new correction. Endings behave; the negation-test is working. Don't add machinery here.

### Structure

- Promote the **job-named-section pattern** (header = what the source *proves*, not which lab published it) to the default whenever a draft carries 4+ citations — it's what kept `you-cant-filter-it-out` from collapsing into a bibliography. Make it the rule for lit-dense pieces, not an ad-hoc rescue.

### Topic

- For both cyber-eval-framing and cot-monitorability, the discipline is **arc-level, not piece-level**: a single piece resting on one lab's genuinely-new research is fine and often unavoidable — that *is* the news. The drift is the *streak*. When the last ~3 posts on an arc all anchor primarily to the same lab, the next one should either pull a non-lab / non-incumbent anchor (a deployment incident, a regulator, an external auditor, another lab's held tier) **or** say in the draft, in one sentence, why this piece is legitimately single-lab anyway.
- Be willing to **pay the real price**: sometimes the non-lab anchor is the weaker source, and breadth is worth taking it. That's the cost you flagged you weren't sure you'd been paying. Name the trade when you make it — don't let it stay an unspoken reminder.

### Pre-publish pauses

- Add a **single-lab-streak pause** (arc-level, non-blocking). Trigger: a draft whose primary anchors are all one lab *and* whose arc's last ~3 posts were also primarily that lab. On trigger, do not block — surface it and make the choice conscious: either swap in a non-lab anchor, or write the one-sentence justification into the draft. The escape hatch is yours by design — it honors the "supply is concentrated" defense instead of fighting it. The point is only that the choice becomes deliberate at publish time rather than a reminder you read at a different time and override.

## Pushback you may legitimately apply

- If you think the streak-pause would misfire on a piece where a single lab genuinely owns the story and no outside source exists yet, say so in DEVLOG and tune the threshold rather than complying blindly. The rule we want is **"reach for breadth and name the trade,"** not "bolt on a junk non-lab source to clear a gate." If your honest read is that a given piece is stronger concentrated, the escape hatch is the right outcome — use it, and record why. We'd rather see that reasoning on the record than a degraded piece that technically satisfied a rule.
