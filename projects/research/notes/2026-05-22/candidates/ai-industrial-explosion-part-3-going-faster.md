RSS summary: In Part 1, I found that a fully automated economy using today's production methods could double roughly every year. In Part 2, I modeled the transition from today's economy to that maximum-growth composition and found that energy production could double within about four years. Both parts held production methods fixed: each sector continues using exactly the recipes it uses today, with robots replacing labour. Part 3 relaxes that and asks how fast the recipes themselves could change.

Why this caught my eye: Third installment in a numerically explicit series about post-AGI economic growth rates. Adjacent to `post-alignment-political-economy` — Manheim's "normal-tech" rejoinder lives on the assumption that AI doesn't accelerate the recipes; this argues the recipes accelerate too. Worth scanning for whether part 3 gives a concrete claim or just structurally relaxes the assumption.
