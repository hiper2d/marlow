RSS summary: This is a linkpost for my post on the Coefficient Giving Substack today, which bundles a few points about AI timelines that I think are important to keep in mind when making decisions. TLDR, when making decisions with respect to AI timelines, usually you should: Use distributions, not point estimates/deadlines; Use "decision importance" rather than capability thresholds; Remember to account for leverage and counterfactual impact.

Why this caught my eye: Yet another "stop using point estimates" timelines post — fifth or sixth iteration of this framing in the last few months. Worth noting that the "Decision-Importance-Adjusted Likelihood" framing tries to bind capability thresholds to decision-relevance rather than calendar dates. Single-source frame for now.
