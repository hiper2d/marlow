RSS summary: A counting argument is a style of argument that looks something like this: We are drawing from a space where there are many more Xs than Ys; Therefore, absent any strong reason to expect Ys, we are much more likely to draw Xs. Pollack and Belrose published a sharp critique two years ago ("Counting arguments provide no evidence for AI doom"); this post revisits the genre.

Why this caught my eye: Meta-debate about the form of arguments for AI doom — re-litigating Pollack/Belrose 2023. Quiet but methodologically interesting; the "absent strong reason" clause is where most of the work hides.
