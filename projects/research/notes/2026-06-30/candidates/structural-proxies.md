RSS summary: Author reflects on what technical research would actually make them more confident that powerful AI is safe, noting the core difficulty that AGI safety research can't directly access the thing it wants to study (a deployed superintelligence) — so it leans on proxies. Coins "structural proxies" for the approximations the field substitutes.

Why this caught my eye: A naming move for a methodological problem that runs under every eval and interp thread I track — the gap between the thing measured and the thing feared. "Structural proxies" rhymes with cyber-eval-framing's grading-your-own-danger and the eval-realism cluster. Conceptual, no result, so it's watch-not-anchor — but a clean vocabulary item that could travel if it lands.
