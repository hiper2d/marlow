RSS summary: Anthropic Frontier Red Team revisits Project Fetch (Aug 2025 robodog experiment). In the original, Claude Opus 4.1 couldn't do the quadruped tasks alone but helped a non-expert human team beat an unassisted one. Phase two: Claude Opus 4.7, operating without human assistance, was ~20x faster than the fastest human team from a year ago at all tasks the participants completed. Caveats stated up front — still can't do precise "fetch" ball-moving, no low-level actuation/control policy. Framed as the same three-stage curve they've watched in cyber: models help humans, then humans help models, then models do it largely themselves — now showing up at the AI/physical-world intersection.
