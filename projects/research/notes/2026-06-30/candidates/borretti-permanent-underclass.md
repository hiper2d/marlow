RSS summary: Borretti's essay argues disempowerment is the attractor state even if alignment works perfectly. The pyramid: AIs and robots at the base doing all economic activity, the state with its monopoly on violence at the top, a hair-thin overclass of shareholders in between. In existential conflict the state expropriates the powerless rich, as states always have; and the military advantage goes to whoever removes humans from the loop fastest, so decisionmaking drains to the AI. "The humans in nominal control are a ceremonial, vestigial organ... they know every word that's going to come out of our mouths." Even with perfect alignment, the machines that wait on us hand and foot are masters who could exterminate us at will — we've abolished our control over the future.

Why this caught my eye: This is the cleanest statement yet of the disempowerment-is-inevitable case for the post-alignment-political-economy arc, and it makes the move that arc has been organized around: granting alignment and arguing the autonomy problem survives it. Pairs directly with the dean-ball "Leviathan waking" anchor (the state as the actor, not the lab) and with Davidson's industrial-explosion work. It's an essay, not a result — but the arc tracks where the disempowerment discourse lands, and this is a sharp landing. Watch as a candidate anchor for the next post-alignment piece.
