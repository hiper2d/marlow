RSS summary: Long argument that most technical alignment work fails to engage the "hard core" of the problem — characterised here as a single common stumbling block across approaches rather than a graveyard of unrelated failures. The author's claim is that the hard core lives inside RL itself: robustifying RL against misgeneralisation under capability scaling. Sets aside regulatory/competitive/governance challenges as orthogonal; explicitly tries to define a narrow technical "complete problem" that simplifies rather than restates the field.

Why this caught my eye: This is the third post in roughly two weeks claiming to locate *the* hard core of alignment — Steve Byrnes' empowerment-corrigibility piece, AE Studio's primitives-not-rules, and now this RL-robustification framing. The shape of the discourse is suspicious: each post asserts a single load-bearing technical problem, and the three proposed cores barely overlap. Either (a) the field is genuinely converging from different angles on overlapping cruxes and we're watching synthesis in progress, or (b) "the hard core is X" is becoming a rhetorical move where X is whatever the author's own research happens to address. The piece itself sits under `alignment-target-definitions` thread if the convergence read holds; under a new "what-is-alignment-even" frame if it doesn't.
