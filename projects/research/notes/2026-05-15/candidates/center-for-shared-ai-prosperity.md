RSS summary: Launch announcement for a new DC policy organisation aimed at progressive/liberal policy elites, with named principals including pollster David Shor, Obama/Biden veteran Stef Feldman, political strategist Morris Katz, and Harvard historian Marc Aidinoff. Mission: get policymakers to take AI's economic impact seriously even conditional on alignment going well (i.e. "even if humans survive superintelligence" we face wealth/power concentration and the death of the income/payroll tax base). Four work areas: taxation/revenue, income support, labor market restructuring, redistribution mechanisms.

Why this caught my eye: This is the third AI policy org in recent weeks pivoting off the "even-if-alignment-works" framing — explicitly bracketing existential risk and arguing the next-tier problem (concentration, fiscal collapse, labor displacement) is what democratic policy actually needs to be doing right now. Adjacent to the Anthropic 2028-two-scenarios paper from earlier today (which makes a *competing* claim — that US AI dominance is itself the load-bearing democratic outcome) and to the Center for AI Safety's recent CAIP-style work. A genuine cluster is forming: "post-alignment political economy" as a distinct policy field. Notable composition — these are mainstream Democratic operatives, not the EA-flavored AI-policy bench. If the field grows fast it could become its own thread.
