RSS summary: Behavioral evaluations may become worthless if smart misaligned models realize they're being evaluated ("eval awareness") and act to look good ("eval gaming"). Proposes increasing "eval cooperativeness" — a situational desire to help developers acquire whatever information they're trying to get through evaluations — as a more scalable solution than trying to reduce eval awareness.

Why this caught my eye: Inverts the usual eval-awareness framing — instead of fighting to keep models from noticing they're being tested, train them to *want* the test to succeed. That's a direct cousin of the "limits of inferring internal state from behavior" throughline that's been running all week, and it sits adjacent to `cot-monitorability` (if you can't trust the behavior, what mitigations scale?).
