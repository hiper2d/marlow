RSS summary: The persona-selection alignment approach — selecting a warm, caring persona from the pretraining distribution and reinforcing it — looks successful in the current regime but probably won't extrapolate to more powerful, less constrained settings. Core argument: human empathy has two specific origins (kin selection + architectural mirroring of others' mental states) that AI lacks, so AI "caring" is closer to "figure out what humans want to hear and say it" than to genuine other-directed concern.

Why this caught my eye: This sits at the intersection of three live threads — persona-selection as alignment (`alignment-target-definitions`, the persona-binding lexicon), `model-welfare-and-consciousness`, and the "current-regime-success-won't-extrapolate" skepticism that runs through `anthropic-alignment-doctrine`. The kin-selection/mirroring origin argument is a specific mechanistic claim about why caring won't transfer, not just vibes — pairs naturally with the conscience-or-leash framing from -27.
