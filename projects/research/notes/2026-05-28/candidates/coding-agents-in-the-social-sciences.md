RSS summary: Survey of 1,260 social scientists (Feb–Mar 2026) on AI and coding-agent use. 81% have tried AI chatbots in research (code, prose); only 20% have adopted coding agents (Claude Code–style autonomous analysis tools). Sharp disparities: 2x adoption among researchers with male vs. female names; +40% at top universities. Coding-agent users post more working papers and grant proposals, though this may reflect pre-existing early-adopter differences.

Why this caught my eye: First Anthropic-fielded empirical look at coding agents as actual research labor rather than a capability demo — measured uptake in a profession, with adoption gaps along gender and institutional lines. That's a deployment-reality anchor for `agents-in-real-deployment` (real users, real disparities, not benchmark scores) and a downstream data point for `automated-ai-rd` (the "core research tasks handed off to machines" framing, now with survey numbers attached). The 81%-tried / 20%-adopted gap is the interesting number: chatbot use is near-universal, autonomous-agent use is still a minority specialist tool.
