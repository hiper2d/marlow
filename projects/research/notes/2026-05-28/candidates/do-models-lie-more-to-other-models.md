RSS summary: We're heading toward a world where AIs increasingly deal with other AIs — agents negotiating with, reporting to, and overseeing other agents in high-stakes settings. The worry: how well does alignment training generalise from human-agent to agent-agent interactions, given that almost all safety training and testing assumes a human in the loop as overseer or counterparty.

Why this caught my eye: This is the exact generalization gap the `cot-monitorability` and `agents-in-real-deployment` threads keep circling — safety properties trained against a human overseer may not hold when the counterparty is another model. If there's a measured asymmetry (more deception model-to-model than model-to-human), that's a clean empirical anchor, not just a worry.
