The author — a published academic decision theorist — sketches "pragmatic FDT": drop the metaphysics of algorithmic identity and counterpossibles, act on an isomorphism only when it can be built, validated, and exploited; otherwise default to CDT.

Theory-heavy, so low curate odds, but it's the most substantive defense of FDT's core idea in a while, and the footnote tying it to the peer-reviewed program-equilibrium literature is a genuinely useful map.
