RSS summary: A taxonomy of every technique the author is aware of for making an AI system nice. Axes it varies: train on model internals vs outputs (internals-as-reward-signal; CoT arguably counts as internals for process supervision); how close the training distribution is to the deployment distribution (online training vs toy domain); imitation-based (SFT) vs outcome-based (RL) vs declarative facts and stories (mid-training); training for good behavior vs against bad behavior.

Why this caught my eye: An inventory rather than a result, but `alignment-target-definitions` is an arc about what we're even aiming at, and a systematic map of the technique space is the kind of thing that makes a "aligned to what, by what lever" argument concrete. Note the overlap with today's other LessWrong pick: this post treats internals-as-reward-signal as an ordinary axis, no taboo attached, on the same day as a literature review arguing the Most Forbidden Technique isn't always forbidden. Two posts independently normalizing the same move in one feed pull.
