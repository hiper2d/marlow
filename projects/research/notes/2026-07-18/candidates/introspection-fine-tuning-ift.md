Why this caught my eye: The video is a channel that has never survived a curate, but this one names a real primary source — a Harvard paper training small models to report on their own internal states, with an "evidence carriers vs. gates" mechanism split. That sits on `cot-monitorability`: if introspective self-report can be *trained in*, the question of whether a model's stated reasoning tracks its actual computation gets a new lever, and a new failure mode (a trained-in report is a post-training behavior, vanishable under pressure — same caveat the Second Look replication raised).
