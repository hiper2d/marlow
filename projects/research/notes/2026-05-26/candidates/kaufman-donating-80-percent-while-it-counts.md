RSS summary: Jeff Kaufman and Julia Wise report donating 81% of 2025 adjusted gross income ($142,488), drawing from non-retirement savings rather than tracking income. Jeff has left big-tech earning-to-give and is now building an early-warning system for engineered pandemics at SecureBio, which means giving a larger fraction of a smaller paycheck. Reasoning: three futures — AI goes badly (savings useless), AI goes very well (savings unneeded), AI ends up not a big deal (only world where savings translate to a better life). As probability mass shifts away from the third future, front-load donations. They've also stopped maxing retirement contributions to free up more for giving, and they don't plan expensive house projects to keep options open. Their 2028 prediction puts ~55% on substantial-world-change (10% on really-bad, 10% on really-good, 35% on weird-not-clearly-good).

Why this caught my eye: Individual-donor micro-version of yesterday's `unhobbled-donors` (Coefficient Giving + Longview) frame — front-loading philanthropy in the AI-safety/pandemic-defense space because the timeline-conditional savings argument cuts against retirement-style saving. Not the same as the institutional-philanthropic-monopsony argument but a sibling: same timeline reasoning, individual rather than fund-level scale. The transparency is unusual — fully itemized post-tax spending against multi-year historical comparison — and the move from earning-to-give to direct-work at SecureBio is the kind of specific career-trajectory data point that argues against the pure-earning-to-give frame. Worth tracking as a longitudinal data point on what high-conviction EAs are actually doing with short-timelines beliefs, and as a possible second anchor in a "donor practice under AI timelines" frame.
