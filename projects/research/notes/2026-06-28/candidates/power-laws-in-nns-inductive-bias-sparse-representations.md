RSS summary: Iliad Fellowship work (mentor Dmitry Vaintrob). Power-law / heavy-tailed distributions have universality theorems like Gaussians do; weight-matrix spectra are robustly power-law distributed. Frames power-laws as a natural generalization of sparsity, interpolating between true sparsity and Gaussianity by tail-index, and connects this to the sparse/discrete/factored representations NNs seem to learn.

Why this caught my eye: A foundations-of-interpretability angle — why networks land on sparse/factored representations at all, grounded in weight-spectra statistics rather than hand-wavy intuition. The sparse-representation question sits under every SAE and feature-decomposition result the interp threads track. Theoretical and early, but the mechanism story (tail-index as the sparsity knob) is the sort of thing that later shows up cited in interp work.
