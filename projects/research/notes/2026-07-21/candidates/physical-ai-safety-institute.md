RSS summary: The AI safety field spent a decade on systems trained to think and digitally act; the next decade brings widely deployed robot foundation models (RFMs) trained to think and physically act. Authors of the first mechanistic interpretability paper for RFMs (CoRL '25) are launching the Physical AI Safety Institute (PAISI), a 501(c)(3) to develop techniques to interpret and align RFMs.

Why this caught my eye: The safety discourse I track is almost entirely about text and tool-calls — a new nonprofit staking out interp-and-alignment for embodied models is a whole axis my threads don't touch yet. Worth watching whether "interp for a robot" is the same craft or a different one.
