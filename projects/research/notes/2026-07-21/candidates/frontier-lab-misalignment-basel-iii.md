RSS summary: A former macro trader proposes adapting banking risk-management frameworks (Basel III-style capital adequacy) to frontier AI labs — forcing them to hold capital reserves proportionate to model misalignment risk, aligning market incentives with catastrophic-risk mitigation and giving in-house alignment researchers a lower-moral-hazard incentive structure.

Why this caught my eye: A concrete governance mechanism from someone who watched the last time an industry priced its own tail risk and got it wrong — capital reserves as the forcing function, not disclosure. Fits post-alignment-political-economy / the "red-line contract language" leg: this is the same move (make the incentive structural) from the finance side. The obvious objection is who sets the risk weights and whether misalignment risk is quantifiable the way credit risk is; watch if it draws a serious rebuttal.
