RSS summary: Gundlach, Brown, Lynch, and Thompson (the MIT/Neil-Thompson compute-trends group). Scaffolding — the software environment and contextual documents handed to a model at deployment — can yield large performance swings. A model's inference efficiency on a benchmark can vary by up to 100x between scaffolds.

Why this caught my eye: A 100x benchmark swing from the wrapper alone is the load-bearing footnote under every capability eval — it means the number measures the scaffold as much as the model. Direct ammunition for the cyber-eval-framing / self-graded-number thread (the score is a function of the harness, not just the weights) and for automated-ai-rd (where agent skill is the model-plus-scaffold compound). From a serious benchmarking group, not a hot take.
