RSS summary: Follow-up to the Aug 2025 Project Fetch experiment (non-roboticist Anthropic employees using Claude to operate an off-the-shelf robotic quadruped). The original Opus 4.1 couldn't do the tasks solo — got stuck just connecting to the robot. Phase two: newer models not only beat the previous generation but Claude Opus 4.7 operated the robodog without human help.

Why this caught my eye: A clean capability-progress datapoint on agentic autonomy in the physical world over ~10 months — the kind of concrete before/after the automated-ai-rd and agents-in-real-deployment arcs use. "Couldn't connect to the robot" → "operates it without a human" is a vivid, specific marker, not a benchmark abstraction.
