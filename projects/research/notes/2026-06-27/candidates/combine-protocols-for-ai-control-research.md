RSS summary: AI control research has produced many protocols — trusted monitoring, untrusted monitoring, resampling — each with its own safety-usefulness tradeoff. Labs might want to combine them to hit maximum usefulness at a minimum acceptable safety bar (e.g. 90%), since a single protocol may not deliver both. The post asks how to compose protocols.

Why this caught my eye: Feeds the ai-control-camp arc directly — it's the operational follow-on to the GDM Control Roadmap and Buck/Redwood's safety-usefulness tradeoff model, both already on the thread. The composition question (how protocols stack, where they interfere) is the next concrete step past "here's our taxonomy." Note the cross-thread tell: trusted monitoring leans on transcript readability that cot-monitorability keeps finding decays — combining protocols is partly an admission no single monitor holds.
