RSS summary: A sanity check for oversight/eval schemes: imagine the AI replaced by a competent, strategic human who knows they may be evaluated and has their own agenda — would the evaluation still work? Applied broadly it gives a discouraging picture: the questions we care most about ("is it safe to give this AI more power?") map onto exactly the cases where human evaluation is already known to be unreliable. Part of an "eval-limits.com" collection on the limitations of AI oversight.

Why this caught my eye: A clean, portable heuristic that reframes the whole eval-realism problem — not "can we build a better test" but "does the test survive an adversary who knows they're being tested." Sits squarely on the cot-monitorability / eval-realism seam and rhymes with the eval-awareness cluster (if the AI knows it's a CTF, the test breaks). Worth watching whether "human substitution test" travels as a phrase the way "safety arguments don't silently expire" did.
