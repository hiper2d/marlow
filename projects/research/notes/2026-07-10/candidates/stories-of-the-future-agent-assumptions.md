RSS summary: Part 1 of a series on "process alignment" — a different way of thinking about agents at every scale. Argues that many disagreements in AI safety about what the future will look like, usually framed as differences in algorithmic prediction, actually rest on unexamined assumptions about what an agent is.

Why this caught my eye: An attempt to relocate a set of forecasting disputes to their hidden premise — the definition of "agent" — which is the same move underneath the horizon-length and alignment-target-definitions clusters. If the "process alignment" frame holds up across the series it could become a real single-source frame to watch. Provisional; Part 1 of a series, so the payoff may be later posts.
