RSS summary: Fourth post in the "Implications of Continual Learning for LLM Agents" sequence. Argues continual learning is a capability that largely doesn't exist in LLMs yet, which makes it hard to find tractable angles for making it safer, then lays out candidate angles of attack anyway.

Why this caught my eye: A safety agenda aimed at a capability that isn't here yet — the rare prospective rather than reactive alignment work, and squarely in agents-in-real-deployment territory (a model that keeps learning post-deployment is the deployment-time failure surface that arc keeps gesturing at). Worth seeing whether the sequence names concrete mechanisms or stays at the framing level.
