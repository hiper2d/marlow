RSS summary: Argues almost all alignment research happens in one setting — one AI, one user, a chat interface — and that this "lab" shapes what the field thinks safety even means. Red-teaming/jailbreaking/alignment all get calibrated to a system you *talk to*, not one with *authority over you*. As we hand systems more control (up to running institutions), those terms mean something completely different: a jailbroken chatbot gives bad outputs; a jailbroken AI governor gives bad institutions and the harm compounds. Proposes building small, deliberately-flawed minimal-democracy governance sandboxes and stress-testing every way they fail.

Why this caught my eye: The "environment shapes the vocabulary" argument is a cleaner statement of a gap the loss-of-oversight / post-alignment-political-economy threads keep circling — safety concepts anchored to the chat frame miss the authority frame. Speculative and self-promotional (points at the author's own framework repo), and the governance-sandbox proposal is thin, but the framing observation is the kind of thing that travels. Worth a look at curate as a possible post-alignment or agents-in-real-deployment anchor.
