RSS summary: Argues from child-rearing: we align children exogenously (operant conditioning, rewards and punishments) and it works because children have instinctive behaviors that make them alignable. But we don't align adults that way — an adult who strays isn't retrained, they're removed from society. We expect adults to be *endogenously* aligned: to keep themselves in line by knowing what's expected and having emotional responses (fear, shame, guilt) that motivate meeting it. Notes the failure mode: people specification-game and hide misaligned actions rather than correct them. Gestures at a fourth mode beyond the three emotions.

Why this caught my eye: Feeds `alignment-target-definitions` — another distinct fork on what the target actually is, this time "internal self-regulating state" rather than behavior or values. Two things make it more than the usual human-analogy post: it names emotional self-control as the mechanism, which puts it next to the Anthropic emotion-vectors result on cot-monitorability, and it flags concealment as the built-in failure of exactly that mechanism. That's the obfuscation arc's core problem restated as a property of the alignment target rather than the monitor. Analogy-driven and no experiment, so it's a framing anchor, not evidence.
