RSS summary: Follow-up to the author's "Why I left Google DeepMind" post. Where that one told a story, this is mechanism design and negotiation posture — the actual contract language he drafted for Google Cloud's government AI deals. Four criteria: good red lines (rule out autonomous targeting without human control and untargeted profiling, allow missile defense; avoid the weaknesses legal analysts flagged in Anthropic's red lines), robust red lines (Cloud pushes deals through any loophole, Legal wouldn't tighten it, the Pentagon doesn't want terms at all), minimal trust assumptions (Chief Scientist as the single root of trust, staffing a Review Body), accountability via transparency.

Why this caught my eye: The `why-i-left-google-deepmind` anchor was the resignation; this is the artifact he was resigning over, published. That's rare — most "labs and the state" commentary argues about what red lines *should* say, and this one is a person who had to write language that survives a counterparty who wants no terms at all, and says which parts held.
