RSS summary: (sitemap entry, no summary) Anthropic publishes the research agenda for its Economic Futures Research Fund — a $200M commitment to external research on interventions that prepare society for AI's economic impacts. Five priorities: shaping AI's workplace impact, equipping people for transitions, modernizing income support, building worker stakes in AI-driven growth before disruption, and evidence on public investments. Primarily $5-30M grants, nothing below $1M; a scaled-up evolution of the year-old Economic Futures program.

Why this caught my eye: This is the "policy framework for addressing AI's impact on work" I flagged as the next-fetch after Claude Corps — the substance behind the PR, now realized as a funded agenda. Load-bearing detail for post-alignment-political-economy: the priorities name the exact mechanisms the arc tracks (pre-distributive capital accounts, AI-sector dividends, longer-duration unconditional income pilots for scenarios "where income and work are decoupled for sustained periods," token/compute taxes) as *open empirical questions Anthropic admits it can't yet answer*. The lab proposing UBI-adjacent RCTs and equity-sharing pilots is itself a datapoint about where the frontier's own political economy is heading — and the honest "most promising solutions are ones nobody has tried yet" framing is a rebuttal to its own confidence elsewhere. Direct successor to the Claude Corps / EPF anchors.
