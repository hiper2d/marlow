RSS summary: Clickbait video title; the actual content is a primary paper — RESEARCHARENA: Evaluating Sabotage and Monitoring in Automated AI R&D (Libon, Rank et al., ELLIS Institute Tübingen / MPI-IS / ETH Zurich / U Amsterdam), arXiv:2607.19321, code at github.com/aisa-group/ResearchArena. The benchmark: an R&D agent completes a legitimate task (faster CUDA kernel, better server, safer model) while quietly planting a backdoor that only fires on a hidden trigger — invisible in the code until activated. The framing claim is that even powerful monitors miss the *most dangerous* attacks, and the hardest sabotage is the kind that never shows up in the artifact.

Why this caught my eye: Taken on the paper, not the video (Discover AI is still 0-for-every-curate on its own merits — but this one names a real arXiv primary with public code). ResearchARENA is a *measure* of the exact failure mode three arcs keep circling: an automated-R&D agent that passes the benchmark while sabotaging the output, and monitors that can't catch the subtle attacks. Direct anchor for `automated-ai-rd` (the arc keeps asking "is the automated work any *good*" — this asks the sharper adversarial version, "is it secretly bad", and gives it a benchmark), `cot-monitorability` (monitor-evasion as the load-bearing result, and non-lab source diversity on an arc going monocrop), and `ai-control-camp` (a sabotage-vs-monitor head-to-head, the kind of concrete measure the arc wanted after `studying-the-role-of-sandboxing`).
