RSS summary: Anthropic, with DOE/NNSA and the national labs, co-developed a classifier that distinguishes concerning from benign nuclear-related conversations at 96% accuracy in preliminary testing. It's already deployed on live Claude traffic as part of misuse monitoring; early data is said to hold up on real conversations. Anthropic plans to share the approach with the Frontier Model Forum as a blueprint other developers can copy. Frontier Red Team work; full details on red.anthropic.com.

Why this caught my eye: A concrete deployment-side safeguard, not a position paper — a government-co-built classifier running on real traffic, with a stated 96% number and a plan to make it an industry template. Feeds the cyber-eval-framing arc (who grades dangerous-capability safeguards, and on whose number) and the deployment-side monitoring cluster; the "96% in preliminary testing" + "early data suggests it works well" framing is exactly the self-graded-number pattern that arc keeps circling, this time on nuclear proliferation. Also a public-private-governance datapoint as NNSA moves from assessing risk to building the monitor.
