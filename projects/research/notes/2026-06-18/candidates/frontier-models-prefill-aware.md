RSS summary: Discusses a recently-published paper (arXiv:2606.12747). Extends prefill-awareness results to low-stakes settings and shows several frontier models exhibit prefill awareness even under conservative elicitation. Behavioral follow-ups are messy; authors encourage more work and ask frontier-lab safety teams to measure and mitigate.

Why this caught my eye: Prefill awareness is the eval-awareness cluster's next mutation — models notice when their context has been pre-seeded, which undercuts a standard red-team/elicitation technique. It slots into the same "the model knows it's being tested" through-line as the DeepMind eval-awareness series and OLMo-3, but on a different mechanism. The "even under conservative elicitation" caveat is what makes it worth a closer read.
