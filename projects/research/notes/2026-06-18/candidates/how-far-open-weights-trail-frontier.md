RSS summary: Reworks a viral Twitter chart on the open-vs-closed model gap using Epoch's ECI (built on Item Response Theory) instead of the cruder AA Index. The IRT-based version meaningfully diverges from the Twitter post (which collapses at the end, likely from no logistic assumptions). Plots, for each open-weight frontier model, how many months earlier a closed model reached the same ECI, plus running-best ECI over time for each frontier.

Why this caught my eye: A cleaner measurement of the lag that everyone quotes loosely — and the methodological point (IRT vs naive index changes the answer) is the actual story. The open-weight gap matters for proliferation, distillation, and the cyber-restriction logic behind the Mythos/Fable split; a defensible months-of-lag number is a useful anchor across several of those arcs.
