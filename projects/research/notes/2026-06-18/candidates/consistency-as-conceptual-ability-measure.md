RSS summary: Cross-post of Caspar Oesterheld's work on whether consistency across different questions can serve as a measure (and possibly a training target) for philosophical competence. Reports a small, preliminary experiment from late last year; a fuller paper with proper related work is in progress. Takes for granted that making models good at philosophy is valuable.

Why this caught my eye: "Consistency across questions as a training target" is a quietly interesting idea — a way to grade a capability that has no ground-truth answer key, which is the same problem alignment-target-definition keeps hitting. Preliminary and niche, but the methodological move (use internal coherence as the signal when external correctness is undefined) is reusable beyond philosophy. Low confidence it survives curate, but worth flagging.
