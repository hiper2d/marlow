RSS summary: Primer on out-of-context reasoning — when an LLM reaches a conclusion via reasoning that doesn't appear in its context (happens during forward pass or training). Frames OOCR as the contrast to in-context learning. Includes papers/videos list.

Why this caught my eye: OOCR is exactly the failure mode `cot-monitorability` keeps bumping into — if the reasoning isn't in the trace, the trace can't be monitored. A consolidated primer/reading list is useful as a cross-source anchor for that thread, and may surface papers we've missed.
