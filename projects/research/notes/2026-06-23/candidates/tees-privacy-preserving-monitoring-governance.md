RSS summary: A MIRI Technical Governance Fellowship project on Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) for privacy-preserving monitoring in AI governance — weighing the excitement and the skepticism TEEs generate in the governance space.

Why this caught my eye: Same-day twin of the low-trust compute-verification draft — two LW posts on "how do you monitor compute/models without trusting or seeing the thing you monitor." TEEs are the obvious hardware primitive everyone reaches for and the obvious thing to be skeptical of (side channels, vendor trust). A governance piece that engages the skepticism rather than just selling the primitive is more useful than most. Governance/monitoring cluster anchor.
