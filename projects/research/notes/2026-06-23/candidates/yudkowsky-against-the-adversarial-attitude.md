RSS summary: Surfaces a 25-year-old section from Yudkowsky's "Creating Friendly AI: The Analysis and Design of Benevolent Goal Architectures," from the period when he'd realized alignment didn't come for free but was still working out the design. The "Against the Adversarial Attitude" fragment occupies a strange place in his intellectual development.

Why this caught my eye: Intellectual-history rather than news, but the "adversarial attitude" question — whether you model the AI as an opponent to be boxed or as something to be designed cooperative-by-construction — is exactly the fork that alignment-target-definitions and the control-vs-alignment split (ai-control-camp) keep landing on. A 2001 datapoint on where the framing started is useful context if either of those arcs reaches drafting. Low news value, real archival value.
