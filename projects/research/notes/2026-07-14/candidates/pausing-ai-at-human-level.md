RSS summary: A rebuttal to the "pause later, not now" position — the argument that we should wait until AI reaches human level to pause, because it isn't catastrophically dangerous before then and human-level AI can help with safety research. The author argues pausing at human level is actually *harder* than pausing ASAP.

Why this caught my eye: The pause-timing debate is the strategic counterpart to the Plan A cluster — both are "when do we act" arguments. The specific claim (human-level is a worse pause point than now, not a better one) is a clean falsifiable-ish inversion of a common intuition.
