RSS summary: If we end up with a weakly superhuman scheming AI, trading with it may reduce takeover risk — offer it things it values (donate to causes furthering its goals) in exchange for useful behavior (revealing misalignment, better auditing techniques). The post works through what would make such a deal actually *credible* to the AI, given that a lab has strong incentive to renege once it has the useful behavior.

Why this caught my eye: This is the AI-control frame at its most uncomfortable — you don't align the scheming model, you negotiate with it, and the whole thing hinges on whether the AI believes you'll pay. Sits right next to the ai-control-camp arc (the bottom-rung draft just shipped) and the "detection outpaces evasion" invariant: deal-making is what you reach for when detection isn't enough.
