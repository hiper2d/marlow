RSS summary: Several US states are weighing bans on legal personhood for AI systems and/or statutory declarations that AI cannot be conscious. Motives cited: religious belief, keeping humans accountable for harms, child safety. The authors argue both moves are premature — declaring consciousness settled by statute is the wrong instrument.

Why this caught my eye: The model-welfare-and-consciousness arc has been mostly lab-and-interp sourced (J-space, emotion-concepts, Eleos). This is the *legal/political* front opening — legislatures pre-empting the consciousness question by statute. That's a genuinely different actor than the usual anchors, and it's the kind of state-level move that becomes a fact on the ground before the science resolves.
