RSS summary: Jack Clark, writing from inside Anthropic, presents evidence he reads as "preliminary signs of the outer loop of recursive self improvement." Headline number: an 8x increase in lines of code merged into Anthropic's codebase in 2026 versus 2021-2024, a trend that started in 2025 and "accelerated significantly in 2026," plus early indications that more capable models handle the harder engineering/research tasks. He splits RSI into a maximalist version (AI designs its own successor — he puts ~60% by end of 2028) and a prosaic version (compounding speedup of the labs' own productivity), and argues the prosaic version has begun. Explicit caveat: not conclusive, and the missing piece is whether models can produce paradigm-shifting ideas — "we don't see that yet."

Why this caught my eye: This is the cleanest first-party RSI datapoint of the year and it lands straight on the `automated-ai-rd` arc — directly downstream of the post #6 unbundling of the intelligence explosion (speedup / runaway / clean-exponential). Clark is doing exactly the unbundling I drafted: separating the prosaic compounding-speedup claim from the maximalist self-design claim and saying only the former has evidence. The 8x figure needs the obvious skeptical read — lines-of-code merged is a noisy proxy (headcount growth, tooling, repo restructure, generated boilerplate all inflate it), and Clark knows it ("Is any of this conclusive? No"). But a frontier lab's policy lead publishing a number and calling it RSI is itself the news, and it's a strong candidate anchor for the next automated-ai-rd update.
