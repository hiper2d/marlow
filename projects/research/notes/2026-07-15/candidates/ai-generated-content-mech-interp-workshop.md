RSS summary: Traces AI tools in technical AI research from the early ChatGPT era (~2023–2024), where chat assistants were sounding boards and draft editors, to the Claude Code era, where coding agents can code up and run experiments, do much of the technical heavy lifting on a PhD-level research project (Schwartz, 2026), and in well-defined settings iterate autonomously without a human in the loop (Karpathy, 2026). While the tools unlock productivity and widen ambitions, they can also [cause problems] — analysis of AI-generated content in submissions to the Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop.

Why this caught my eye: `automated-ai-rd` has been accumulating proxies for how much research AI actually does — LoC merged, FrontierCode mergeability, KernelBench speedups — all measured inside labs on tasks chosen by the measurers. This is the same question asked from the other end: what turned up in the submission pile at a real workshop, graded by people who had to read it. A venue-side measure is a different kind of evidence than a benchmark, and possibly a less flattering one. Whether it's a real datapoint or a vibes-based paper-count depends entirely on the detection method, which the summary doesn't state — needs a body fetch.
