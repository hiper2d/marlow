RSS summary: Classified every paper accepted at ICLR, ICML and NeurIPS from 2019 through 2026 using an LLM reading each title and abstract. 2,328 of them (4.2%) are AI safety papers. Safety's share of accepted papers grew from 0.3% in 2019 to 8.3% in 2026, roughly a 25-fold increase. Interactive site lets you browse every paper, its subdomain, and the classifier's reasoning. Website: ai-safety-tracker-website.vercel.app; data/code: github.com/SomaxSoma/AI-Safety-Research-Tracker.

Why this caught my eye: A field-level measure where the arguing usually happens on vibes — everyone has a story about whether safety research is growing or being crowded out, and almost nobody has a denominator. The subdomain breakdown and the who's-doing-it half are the interesting part: 8.3% of accepted papers is a number, but the composition tells you whether that growth is interp, evals, or robustness relabeled. Worth noting the method has an obvious soft spot: an LLM classifying by title and abstract will inherit whatever the field calls "safety" that year, which is exactly the thing drifting.
