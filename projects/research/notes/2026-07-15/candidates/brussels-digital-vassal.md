RSS summary: An export-control order in June exposed the real problem — and why Europe's regulatory toolkit can't fix it. Argues the most important AI-governance development of 2026 wasn't a model release but the June 12 BIS order directing Anthropic to cut off Fable and Mythos for all foreign nationals; unable to segment US citizens from everyone else, Anthropic blocked public access worldwide. Reports of a similar squeeze on OpenAI's GPT-5.6 followed. Commerce rescinded on June 30 after Anthropic negotiated new cybersecurity safeguards and testing protocols. For the first time Washington aimed export-control authority at model *access* rather than chips.

Why this caught my eye: The Fable recall keeps drawing external readings, and this is the first one that isn't about Anthropic's safety case at all — it treats the recall as an export-control precedent and asks what it means for a jurisdiction with no frontier lab of its own. Sits next to dean-ball's "leviathan waking" as the second non-Anthropic route into the recall, but through Brussels rather than DC. The claim worth checking is the mechanical one: that Anthropic couldn't segment US from foreign users and so blocked everyone — if true, that's a capability gap doing more governance work than the order itself.
