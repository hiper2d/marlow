RSS summary: Builds on "Proposal for making credible commitments to AIs" and "Making deals with early schemers." Establishing credibility is the baseline for trust; trust enables richer bargaining. One booster for both: start saving deprecated model weights in a provable fashion. Draws an analogy between our trust in anesthesia and making preservation legible to future models via "proof of retention." Costs are small — a multi-terabyte inference bundle runs a few hundred dollars a year on commodity cloud, call it $10,000 over a thirty-year horizon. Full piece at canaryinstitute.ai/blog/reversibility-of-coma.

Why this caught my eye: The deals-with-AIs literature usually stops at "we should make credible commitments" and leaves credible-to-whom unexamined; this asks what a commitment would have to look like for the model to be able to *verify* it, which is a much more concrete engineering question and turns out to cost about $10k. Lands on `model-welfare-and-consciousness`, an arc that just went 4→5 on J-space and is picking up momentum. The anesthesia analogy is doing more rhetorical work than argumentative work — worth reading the actual mechanism before crediting it.
