RSS summary: ARC + AIcrowd launch a contest to improve white-box estimation algorithms for random MLPs, building on ARC's recent wide-random-MLP paper. Warm-up round this week; later rounds carry a prize pool of $100k+. Goal: minimize MSE on the expected output of Gaussian-weight MLPs under FLOP constraints, betting that the best methods will be mechanistic rather than black-box sampling.

Why this caught my eye: ARC is explicit that this is a stepping stone toward white-box methods that don't rely on honeypots a smart system can dodge — and they're openly testing whether LLMs can hill-climb their own core research metric, which doubles as a small capability/recursion data point. The "designing performant white-box estimation is hard even for tiny models" admission is the honest negative-result texture worth tracking.
