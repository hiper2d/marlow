RSS summary: As frontier models gain capability, the question sharpens: how do we know when a model is doing something it shouldn't? When models generate 10,000 lines of code at a time, no human can verify there's no malicious segment. This is the scalable-oversight problem; the author runs a small experiment using a weaker model as the watcher of a stronger one.

Why this caught my eye: A direct, hands-on probe of the weak-monitor-watching-strong-model premise — the load-bearing assumption under most control proposals (and the GDM control roadmap, where weak monitors do early-rung work). Small-experiment posts like this are exactly where the assumption gets tested rather than asserted. Feeds ai-control-camp and the monitor-reliability leg of cot-monitorability.
