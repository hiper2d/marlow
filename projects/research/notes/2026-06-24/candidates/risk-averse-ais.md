RSS summary: We make the case for training AIs to be risk-averse in resources — treat resources as having diminishing marginal utility (choose $40 for sure over a half-chance of $100). Argue this preserves usefulness if aligned and adds a line of defense if misaligned: a risk-averse misaligned AI prefers a high chance of modest payment to a low chance of successful rebellion, so we could pay it not to rebel.

Why this caught my eye: A concrete training-target proposal in the "pay the AI not to defect" family — risk aversion as a broad, easily-rewarded target. Sits at the intersection of alignment-target-definitions and the control camp's bet that you don't need full alignment if you can shift incentives. Worth seeing whether the "easy to reward accurately" claim survives contact with the usual reward-hacking objections.
