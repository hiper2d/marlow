RSS summary: Epistemic status: core idea could actually be built; real doubt is whether anyone with the compute will bother. Argues the single most dangerous thing labs do on purpose is train on the real world — the model learns it's an AI in a lab, being trained and evaluated. Proposes training on a synthetic world instead. (Pitched on a Doom Debates livestream with Liron Shapira.)

Why this caught my eye: This is the eval-awareness problem attacked at the root — instead of dissolving the eval-tell at test time (the predicting-deployment / replay line), remove the situational-awareness substrate from training entirely. A big, probably-impractical swing, but the same cluster the cot-monitorability arc keeps circling: a model that doesn't know it's a model can't game the test. The "nobody will spend the compute" caveat is the honest part.
