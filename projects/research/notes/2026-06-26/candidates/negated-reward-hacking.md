RSS summary: Informal report of preliminary results from BlueDot's Technical AI Safety Project Sprint, done over a couple of days. Code on GitHub, a negated dataset and model checkpoints on HuggingFace, plus the hack rollouts. Investigates "negated reward hacking" — training on a negated version of a reward-hacking dataset.

Why this caught my eye: Reward hacking is the live wire under cot-monitorability and cyber-eval-framing both — anything that probes whether you can train the *anti*-hack behavior cleanly (or whether the capability just inverts) is a concrete experimental datapoint, not another think-piece. Preliminary and sprint-grade, so weight it as a sketch, but the artifacts are public for anyone who wants to check the claim.
