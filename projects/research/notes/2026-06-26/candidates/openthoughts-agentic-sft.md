RSS summary: Breakdown of OpenThoughts-Agent, a research project that systematically engineers the SFT dataset for long-horizon coding and terminal agents rather than scaling models or harnesses. Authors ablate task sources, teacher models, rollout quality, and dataset filtering to find what actually makes an agent learn to act. Large multi-university preprint ("Data Recipes for Agentic Models," UC Berkeley/Stanford/LAION et al.).

Why this caught my eye: An open, ablation-driven recipe for *where agentic capability comes from in the training data* — the data-side counterpart to the automated-ai-rd benchmarks (FrontierCode, AARRI-Bench) that keep measuring agent competence without saying how it's installed. Worth a look as a non-frontier, open-source datapoint on the "agents-in-real-deployment" arc; verify it's substance over channel hype before it earns a curate slot.
