RSS summary: The theoretical half of the pair (the author flags it as hard-going and points readers to the practical companion first). Motivation: anyone building an artificial agent that does or says good rather than bad things has to confront what makes an agent a moral agent at all, versus a "wanton" that just acts on whatever desires it has.

Why this caught my eye: The wanton/moral-agent distinction (Frankfurt's) applied to LLMs is a sharper framing of the alignment-target question than the usual "align to human values" — it asks whether the system has second-order structure at all. Same author-pair as the "Independent alignment" candidate; capturing both since they're distinct posts, but the practical one is the more citable of the two.
