RSS summary: Natural language autoencoders (NLAs) are a mostly-unsupervised method for producing free-form text explanations of LLM activations. The author trained several Qwen3-8B NLAs with different length penalties, subtracting a token cost during RL, and finds explanations can be compressed without hurting reconstruction quality.

Why this caught my eye: NLA is part of the interpretability canon the cot-monitorability arc tracks — a follow-up result that the free-text explanations carry redundancy you can squeeze out. Adjacent to the steering-as-explanations-not-handles question of whether these explanations are real handles on the model. Minor but on-arc.
