RSS summary: Newly founded startup Recursive — building AI systems meant to recursively self-improve — published its first results. Its "automated AI research system" runs the full loop (propose idea → implement → run experiment → validate → pick next experiment) and set new SOTA on NanoChat Autoresearch (train a small LM to highest perf on a small compute budget), NanoGPT Speedrun (train to a target perf as fast as possible), and SOL-ExecBench (optimize GPU kernels toward hardware limits). Clark frames it as "early signs of life on RSI" — symptoms of success on *well-defined, measurable, fast-to-evaluate* objectives; open question is whether it repeats where goals are fuzzy and slow to score.

Why this caught my eye: Directly downstream of the automated-ai-rd arc and a near-exact replay of the Prime Intellect nanoGPT-speedrun anchor (#457) — except now it's a whole startup whose *entire pitch* is the loop. The skeptical read writes itself and Clark gestures at it: every benchmark here is the easy case (clean metric, cheap eval), which is exactly the regime where "RSI" is least informative about real research taste. The arc's recurring tension — narrow-metric gains vs paradigm-changing creativity — gets a clean new datapoint. Pairs with the DeepMind AGI→ASI candidate and the Ajeya/Lee self-sustaining one from the same issue.
