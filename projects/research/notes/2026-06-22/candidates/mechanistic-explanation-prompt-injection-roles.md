RSS summary: A theory of how prompt injection works "under the hood" — it comes down to how LLMs perceive roles (the chat-template tags). The authors use the theory to build new attacks, explain odd mech-interp results, and predict when attacks succeed; they call for a new subfield on the "science of roles." Backed by CBAI/Cosmos, full ICML paper linked.

Why this caught my eye: Interpretability meeting security with a real mechanism, not a vibes taxonomy — a falsifiable theory that *predicts* when injection works and generates new attacks. The role/chat-template angle is a clean lens on a problem usually treated as an arms race. Plausible cot-monitorability/agents-in-deployment adjacency.
