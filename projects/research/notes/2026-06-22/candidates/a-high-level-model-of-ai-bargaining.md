RSS summary: Advanced AIs might be capable of credible commitments unavailable to humans, which they could use when bargaining with each other. Drawing on Schelling's The Strategy of Conflict, "bargaining" is framed broadly — any attempt to resolve a dispute over resources, from algorithmic trading and litigation to diplomacy between national AGI projects.

Why this caught my eye: Multi-agent bargaining between AIs with commitment abilities humans lack — a frame adjacent to the post-alignment-political-economy arc (national AGI projects negotiating norms) and to AI-control questions. More theoretical than the feed usually surfaces; worth flagging as a watch-frame rather than a draft anchor.
