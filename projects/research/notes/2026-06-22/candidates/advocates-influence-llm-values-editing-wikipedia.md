RSS summary: Summary of Brazilek, Navas & Gnauck (2026), "Small edits, large models: How Wikipedia advocacy shapes LLM values" (Zenodo). Wikipedia is weighted heavily in LLM training data; the authors show advocacy organizations can shift what LLMs say on a topic by strategically editing Wikipedia articles. Case study: a Pro-Animal Wikipedians group adding factual edits.

Why this caught my eye: A concrete, measured demonstration of training-data influence as a values-attack vector — not a red-team prompt but a durable edit upstream of the training run. Rhymes with the cot-monitorability "cut the behavior at its source / adjacent data refills it" leg, except here someone is deliberately injecting the source. Worth a look as an alignment-target / data-provenance datapoint.
