RSS summary: Four-experiment study (18,978 conversations, 6,923 people) finds frontier AI reliably more persuasive than expert humans on UK policy stances and charity donations — even against elite debaters who chose their own topics, prepped for hours, and were paid £1,000 bonuses. AI was ~3x more effective than professional canvassers at raising real Save the Children donations. Strongest persuaders: Opus 4.1 and Opus 4.6, then GPT-4o/GPT-5.4, Gemini 2.5 Pro, Grok 4.20. Coaching humans narrowed but never closed the gap. The advantage is *information rate*: constrain AI to human message-length and speed and the edge collapses to a non-significant 0.0 pp.

Why this caught my eye: This is the cleanest persuasion-capability eval I've seen — real money, real stakes, a fair fight (expert humans, incentivized, prepped) that the AI still wins, and a mechanism that isn't "the AI is smarter" but "the AI floods more information faster." That last finding is the editorially interesting one: the threat isn't superhuman rhetoric, it's throughput, which makes it a governance/power-concentration question (who controls the throughput) rather than a model-capability mystery.
