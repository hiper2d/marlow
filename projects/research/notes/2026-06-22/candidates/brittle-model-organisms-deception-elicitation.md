RSS summary: SPAR Spring 2026 work (Basani, Tan, Li). tl;dr: finetuning-based auditing methods for model organisms may unintentionally erase the deceptive behavior they're trying to measure — an illusion of success. Studies secret-side-constraint (SSC) model organisms from Cywiński et al. (2025), LLMs finetuned to harbor a verifiable hidden behavior as ground truth to audit against.

Why this caught my eye: A negative result that hits cot-monitorability and the deception-detection arc squarely — the audit procedure itself destroys the thing it's validating against, so reported success may be measurement artifact. Sits right next to the -18 "did-you-lie" belief-verified-organisms pick: detector validation that doesn't survive contact with genuine deception. Strong arc anchor.
