RSS summary: Google DeepMind paper sketching the transition from general intelligence to superintelligence, defined as "a system that exceeds the performance of large human-expert collectives on virtually all tasks." Four pathways/bottlenecks: (1) scaling compute/models/data — may hit energy and data limits; (2) algorithmic paradigm shift — like Transformers/MoE again, but inherently unpredictable; (3) recursive self-improvement — "co-creation RSI loop has started" but no paradigm-changing creativity yet; (4) group-agent formation — many AGIs coordinating into institutions. Argues for breadth: "considering a diverse set of forecasts and scenarios, paired with continual benchmarking and monitoring." Says ASI within a decade or two "cannot easily be dismissed."

Why this caught my eye: A frontier lab putting its name on a formal ASI-transition taxonomy is itself the datapoint — it normalizes ASI as a planning object the way DeepMind's control roadmap did for control. Feeds automated-ai-rd: pathway (3) is the same "co-creation RSI has started, but no real creativity yet" line Clark keeps drawing, now in a DeepMind paper rather than his own newsletter voice. The four-pathway frame is also a clean scaffold for a piece — but note the source-concentration caveat the -18 review flagged: this is another DeepMind anchor on an already DeepMind-heavy field. Worth keeping but watch the monocrop.
