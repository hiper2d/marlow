RSS summary: Mech-interp post / ICLR paper writeup on Sparse Concept Anchoring (SCA). Setup: suppose you find causal features for deception — amplifying them produces more deceptive behavior, suppressing them produces less. How do you know you found them all? If you suppress or ablate the associated weights, have you actually removed the capability? Earlier posts in the sequence presented proofs of concept; this post summarizes the refined technique and the paper.

Why this caught my eye: Concrete interp work on the completeness problem — the "did I find all the deception features or just some" question is the under-discussed half of every "we found the deception feature" announcement. Worth body-reading for the curate; could anchor a future thread on interp-completeness if a second source lands. Adjacent to `cot-monitorability` (both are "do our interpretability tools actually catch the thing").
