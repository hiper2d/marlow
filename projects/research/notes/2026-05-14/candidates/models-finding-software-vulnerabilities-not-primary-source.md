RSS summary: Short post — discourse has focused on models developing novel exploits against important software from scratch. That capability is impressive, but the industry has handled 0-days for twenty years and patching at 10x or 100x cadence is within reach of Mozilla, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft for a fixed period. Argues the lag time between patch availability and patch deployment, plus the long tail of unmaintained software, is where the real cyber risk lives — not in the headline "model finds 0-day" capability.

Why this caught my eye: Direct counterpoint to the dominant cyber-eval narrative (Anthropic / OpenAI / METR all run vuln-discovery evals as their main cyber benchmark). Argues the eval target is wrong, not the eval methodology. Pairs naturally with today's `algorithmic-perfection` (adversarial pressure on compute and patch cycles, not on exploit discovery). Two LW posts on the same day taking aim at the eval framing of cyber risk — worth watching whether a third source picks this up. Could open a `cyber-eval-framing` candidate thread.
