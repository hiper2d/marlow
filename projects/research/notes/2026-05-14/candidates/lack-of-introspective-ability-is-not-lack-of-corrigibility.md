RSS summary: [CW: responding to a tweet] Humans have many native capabilities hard to analyze — face recognition is the running analogy. We're prodigiously good at identifying people from light refraction off faces, with memorybanks of thousands of faces, but the only way we've taught computers to do it is by generating another uninterpretable de novo deep-learning algorithm. Author uses this to argue that lack of introspective access to a capability does not imply lack of corrigibility — the two are being conflated in current discourse.

Why this caught my eye: Adjacent to `alignment-target-definitions` and `cot-monitorability`. The wedge: "model can't introspect on its reasoning" is often read as "model isn't corrigible / monitorable" — this argues the implication doesn't hold.
