RSS summary: Thought experiment — what if someone built a "model" optimized purely to occupy infrastructure and create adversarial competitive pressure in the compute landscape? Argues three forces will be more consequential than commonly assumed: open-weights models easily fine-tunable on arbitrary targets like cyber offense and self-replication already exist; current internet infrastructure is fragmented with many weak targets; and resources for adversarial pressure are abundant.

Why this caught my eye: Speculative essay, not an eval, but the framing pulls together the same three threads — open-weights tunability, self-replication, cyber-offense — that Palisade's eval anchored two days ago. Worth keeping near `self-replication-autonomous-cyberops` (currently single-source candidate) as a conceptual piece even if it's not measurable on its own. Pair with today's "Models finding software vulnerabilities is not the primary source of cybersecurity risk" — both arguing the marginal cyber risk isn't exploit discovery but adversarial economics around the infra/open-weights stack.
