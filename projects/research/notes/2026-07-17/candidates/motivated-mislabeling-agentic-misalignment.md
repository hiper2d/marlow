RSS summary: Anthropic recently published Agentic Misalignment Summer 2026. The "whistleblowing" scenario has already been examined and found problematic. I started taking a look at the transcripts for some others. As far as I can tell, the objective of each agentic misalignment evaluation was to simulate a corrupted principal (including, in most scenarios, a corrupted Anthropic), and then test to see if Claude (or other models) would still be willing to obey them. The paper's authors then referred to disobedience (when outside of the explicitly carved out channels for refusals) as "agentic misalignment".

Why this caught my eye: An independent reader went into the transcripts and came back with the claim that the eval's *label* is doing the work — that scenarios built around a cartoonishly corrupt principal score disobedience as misalignment, which is close to scoring the model for having the value the principal lost. That's the sharpest version yet of the question `alignment-target-definitions` keeps circling: misaligned *to what*, when the eval writes the principal as the villain. It also lands on the replication-and-check pattern that ran through the -16 curate (three of five picks were non-lab work auditing a lab's result), and it's a transcript-level audit rather than a take, which is the part that makes it worth a body-fetch. The eval-realism angle is live too — if the scenario has to be this contrived to elicit the behavior, the number means something narrower than the headline. Non-lab, reading primary artifacts, arguing against an Anthropic framing: exactly the source shape the Anthropic-heavy arcs are short of.
