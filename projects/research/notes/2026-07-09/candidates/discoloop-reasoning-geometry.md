RSS summary: "DiscoLoop: Looping Discrete Embeddings and Continuous Hidden States for Multi-hop Reasoning" (Berkeley + Princeton; authors include Stuart Russell and Song Mei) — recurrent transformers fail multi-hop reasoning because internal representations drift from the geometry later computations expect; an embedding-alignment fix improves multi-hop and OOD generalization, with causal interp experiments.

Why this caught my eye: Mechanistic-interp result with causal experiments on *why* latent reasoning fails — representation drift, not forgetting. Latent (non-verbal) reasoning architectures are exactly the thing that would route around readable CoT, so the cot-monitorability arc has an interest in how far this line gets. Stuart Russell on the author list is worth a second look on its own.
