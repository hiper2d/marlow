RSS summary: Models often behave dishonestly without acquiring a coherent deceptive disposition. Researchers trained mid-sized models on their own plausible-but-false reasoning; true and false training usually produced nearly identical downstream effects. Even statements contradicting latent knowledge didn't reliably install a "liar" disposition.

Why this caught my eye: This is the cleanest challenge yet to the model-organism-of-deception framing cot-monitorability leans on — if dishonest behavior doesn't ride on a coherent internal deceptive state, then lie-detector and honesty-probe validation (the did-you-lie anchor) has an even shakier target. A negative result on the "disposition" assumption, non-lab, directly on the arc's central say/do question.
