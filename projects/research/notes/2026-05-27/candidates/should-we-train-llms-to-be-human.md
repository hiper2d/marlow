RSS summary: Discusses Binz et al. 2026, which shows post-training makes LLMs less human-like in their responses. Asks whether that drift is intended and whether it's optimal — from a goal-alignment view, drifting away from human behavior could lower the model's ability to model human needs, but parts of human behavior may also be suboptimal targets for alignment.

Why this caught my eye: Sits across `alignment-target-definitions` and the "what is the target?" sub-question that keeps recurring (AE primitives-not-rules, Byrnes empowerment-corrigibility, Bengio Scientist AI). The Binz et al. finding is the empirical anchor: post-training drifts away from human-likeness, so if "human-like" is the alignment target, RLHF is moving the wrong direction. If "human-like" is not the target, the field needs to be more explicit about what is. Either way, useful primary data for the target-definition arc.
