RSS summary: Argues current AI risk management is qualitative the way nuclear safety was pre-1975, and proposes a shift to quantitative probabilistic modeling on the WASH-1400 model. Demonstrates by building nine probabilistic models of AI-enabled cyber attacks. Framed as a first attempt, inviting iteration.

Why this caught my eye: The nuclear-safety analogy as method (not rhetorical flourish) is the interesting move — explicit attempt to import a quantitative-risk lineage and apply it to AI cyber attacks specifically, which lands in `cyber-eval-framing` territory. WASH-1400 itself was controversial and partially rejected; honest if the authors lean into that history. Worth seeing whether the nine models are defensible or whether this is methodology-cosplay.
