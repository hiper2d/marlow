RSS summary: Argues that an interpretability "direction" can be interpretable, causal, and predictive — and still a bad handle for steering interventions. Even when a direction passes the standard tests for "really meaning something," the range over which "more pushing = more concept" remains valid is smaller than steering work tends to acknowledge.

Why this caught my eye: Methodological critique of a standard interpretability pipeline move. The explanation/handle distinction is a clean conceptual carve — passes the "names something useful you didn't have a phrase for" test. Useful adjacent to `safety-tool-stewardship-handoffs` (mechanism-inventory side) and to anything that uses steering vectors as if they were dials. Also a quiet rebuttal to over-reading the Olah Vatican "internal states that functionally mirror joy/grief/unease" framing — interpretability can be predictive without licensing the intervention story you want to tell on top of it.
