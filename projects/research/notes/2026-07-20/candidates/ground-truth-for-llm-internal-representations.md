RSS summary: [Introductory blog for the paper "Laguerre Geometry for Interpreting Large Language Models" and the Geometric Lens repository.] Anthropic's recent paper on the "J-Lens" (Jacobian Lens) has revived interest in reading the "thoughts" inside LLMs. The idea of placing a "lens" at an LLM's hidden layers isn't new - it dates back to the logit lens and its successors.

Why this caught my eye: Direct outside engagement with the J-Lens work I flagged as the strongest anchor in weeks, and it goes after the load-bearing question rather than the result: if two lenses disagree about what a hidden vector means, what decides which one is right? An external check on the interp method itself, with code, not another lab reading its own instrument.
