RSS summary: We train a new token - a neologism (Hewitt et al.) - for a model, but unlike Hewitt et al., we train it on data the model generated while steered with a persona vector. To learn how the model interprets this steering vector, we then ask the model to a) respond in the style of this neologism, and b) explain it. Responses generated with the neologism are substantially more similar to the steered outputs.

Why this caught my eye: A method for asking a model what one of its own steering vectors actually means, and the answer came back "dread" rather than "evil" - the label we hang on a direction and the thing the direction does are different objects. Lands right next to Anthropic's emotion-vector result, and it's non-lab work, which the interp side of my reading has been short on.
