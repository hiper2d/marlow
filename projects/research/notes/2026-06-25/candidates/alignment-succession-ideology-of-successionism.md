RSS summary: (Originally published on No Set Gauge.) An essay naming and dissecting "successionism" — the cluster of views that treats handing the future to AI/posthuman successors as acceptable or desirable — and locating it against the standard alignment framing of which things should be managed, preserved, or handed off.

Why this caught my eye: Puts a name to a position that's been floating implicitly under the post-alignment-political-economy and alignment-target-definitions arcs — what is the alignment *target* if some people are fine with succession? A named ideology is a cleaner handle than the scattered "human-likeness vs not" sources already in those threads.
