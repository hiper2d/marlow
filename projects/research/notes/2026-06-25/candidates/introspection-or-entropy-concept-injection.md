RSS summary: Anthropic recently reported that language models can "introspect" — take a steering vector for a concept like "oceans," inject it into the model's internal activations, and the model reports noticing the injected thought. This post re-runs the experiment on open models and asks whether what's measured is genuine introspection or just an entropy/distribution-shift artifact.

Why this caught my eye: A direct, reproducible re-examination of Anthropic's introspection claim on open-weight models — the exact negative-result-on-an-interp-claim shape the cot-monitorability arc keeps circling (does the internal-state signal mean what the lab says it means). If it holds, it dents a recent first-party interp result; either way it's a falsifiable cross-lab check.
