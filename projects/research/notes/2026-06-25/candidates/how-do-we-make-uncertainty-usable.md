RSS summary: Most AI researchers put at least 5% on advanced AI causing human extinction or extreme disempowerment; Bengio and Hinton put it at 10%+. The post asks why there is so little global response to a risk estimate that high, and how to make that kind of deep uncertainty actually drive decisions rather than stall them.

Why this caught my eye: The governance/risk-communication angle — turning a widely-shared but non-actionable extinction estimate into something policy can use. Lighter than a technical anchor, but it's the recurring "why doesn't the number move anyone" question that sits adjacent to the self-graded-danger framing in cyber-eval. Watch.
