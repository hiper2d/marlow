RSS summary: Author has been using ChatGPT and Anthropic's Excel add-ons for investment workflows. Argues AI for software engineering went novelty-to-ubiquity in ~2.5 years; the bottleneck for the rest of white-collar work has shifted from model capability to organizational adoption inertia. Frames that inertia as a brief window of opportunity for orgs that apply AI in non-obvious ways.

Why this caught my eye: Another first-person diffusion piece, this time from a finance practitioner — concrete claim about the bottleneck shifting from capability to adoption, which feeds the `agents-in-real-deployment` thread we've been watching. AE Studio's two essays last week argued the same shape from a consultancy seat; this is the seat-of-the-user view. If a third independent source lands soon, the thread may finally cross.
