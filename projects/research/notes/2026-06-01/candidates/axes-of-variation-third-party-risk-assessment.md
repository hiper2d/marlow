RSS summary: Distinctions for thinking about "third-party risk assessment" (crediting discussions with Ajeya Cotra and Paul Christiano). Separates developers from stakeholders (governments, public, board, employees) and notes a key auditor role: reviewing confidential info and disclosing only conclusions, not the underlying material. Maps axes of variation across the assessment space.

Why this caught my eye: Clean conceptual scaffolding for the auditing/oversight strand — adjacent to safety-tool-stewardship-handoffs and the third-party-eval debates running through cyber-eval-framing (XBOW/ExploitBench/AISLE methodology fights) and DeepMind's Gram auditor. The "auditor sees confidential info, discloses only conclusions" axis is exactly the structural question those methodology disputes keep circling. Christiano/Cotra provenance raises the priority.
