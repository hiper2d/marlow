RSS summary: A computational-cognitive-neuroscience researcher's full agenda summary. The bet: the first takeover-capable AI (TCAI) will be LLMs augmented with human-like cognitive faculties they currently lack — persistent memory/continuous learning, metacognition for error detection, executive function for long-horizon planning. Argues for a neglected "third option" between prosaic alignment (assume AI is like today's) and agent foundations (assume we know nothing) — carefully predict the *mechanistic form* of the first AI worth getting right, then design interventions for its specific failure modes. Notes existing alignment techniques (RLHF, constitutional/deliberative, character training) will produce *different* final alignment once those cognitive capacities are bolted on.

Why this caught my eye: This is the explicit, agenda-length version of a frame the feed keeps brushing against — that the regime you can align today isn't the regime that matters, because the load-bearing AI is an architecturally different thing. Sits squarely on the `alignment-target-definitions` arc (what exactly are we aligning) and the horizon/capability-forecast cluster.
