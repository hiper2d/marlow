RSS summary: Steven Byrnes argues our intuitive notions of empowerment, agency, manipulation, corrigibility, helpfulness, and culpability are all bound up with a "messed-up" folk ontology of acausal free will. Walks through alignment-literature attempts to give these concepts a "True Name" — null-policy baselines, self-empowerment generalization (Cannell), Vingean agency (Demski), non-optimization-of-final-state (Flint), impact minimization, attainable utility preservation — and finds each inadequate. Closes pessimistic: probably none of these concepts have rigorous True Names useful for technical alignment.
