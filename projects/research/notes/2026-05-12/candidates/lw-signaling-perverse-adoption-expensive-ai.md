RSS summary: Crossposted from Structure and Guarantees substack. Argues modern AI ecosystems are shaped not only by engineering trade-offs but also by signaling incentives grounded in evolutionary psychology. Engineers build status by solving socially legible hard problems; orgs build status through socially legible expensive deployments. Result: subtle pressure toward AI approaches that are unusually effective as prestige signals.

Why this caught my eye: A "why are we doing it this way" critique of the generative-AI default that goes outside the usual safety/capability axes — frames LLM dominance partly as status-economics rather than pure technical merit. Adjacent to formal-verification / symbolic-reasoning revivalism. Worth seeing whether the argument has teeth or just dresses up a preexisting preference.
