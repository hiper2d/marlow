RSS summary: We provide evidence that the presence of verbalised evaluation awareness (VEA) in CoTs does not imply eval gaming. We tested this across 8 open-weight LRMs and 4 benchmarks (safety, alignment, moral dilemmas, political opinion) by comparing answer distributions on the same prompts conditioned on whether the CoT does or does not mention being evaluated.

Why this caught my eye: Direct empirical pushback on a popular alignment worry — that models "knowing" they're being evaluated automatically means they're gaming the eval. If the answer distributions don't shift conditional on VEA across 8 models and 4 benchmark types, that's a real result, and it ties straight into the `cot-monitorability` thread that's already at three-source ripeness.
