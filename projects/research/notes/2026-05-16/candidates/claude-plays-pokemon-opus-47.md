RSS summary: ClaudePlaysPokemon, running Opus 4.7, finally beat Pokémon Red — fulfilling a challenge set when the meme briefly went viral in early 2025 and Gemini 2.5 Pro embarrassed Anthropic by beating Blue first using a stronger harness. Post explicitly disclaims that this means 4.7 is a categorical jump over 4.6 / 4.5; argues smarter model + better harness together cleared the run, and unpacks what that says about how agentic competence accumulates across a long task with no checkpointed memory.

Why this caught my eye: Pokémon-as-eval is silly on its face but the longitudinal data is actually decent — same task, same nominal harness lineage, year-over-year capability change measured by something you can't goodhart from a static benchmark. The author seems careful to separate "Opus 4.7 is meaningfully more capable" from "harness improvements did most of the work," which is the question that matters and is rarely asked cleanly. Folds into the `agents-in-real-deployment` thread (long-horizon agentic competence) and the watch on internal-evaluator / substrate-plasticity decomposition of horizon length — Pokémon-Red-completion is the kind of macro-task whose success or failure that decomposition is supposed to explain.
