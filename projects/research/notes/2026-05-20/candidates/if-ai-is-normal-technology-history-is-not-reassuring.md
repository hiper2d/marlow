RSS summary: Response to the "AI as normal technology" framing (associated with Toby Ord and others). The truism that technology is net-good — even if it creates winners and losers, it improves the world — depends on outcomes for the *end* of humanity, but jumps over the transitions. The post argues history of those transitions is actually not reassuring.

Why this caught my eye: Direct counter to the Narayanan/Kapoor-style "AI is normal technology, don't panic" frame. Sits cleanly in `post-alignment-political-economy` alongside Anthropic 2028, CSAIP, Copernicus-vs-AIpocalypse, and the multi-source x-risk-bracketing wave from last week. The "transitions" framing is potentially load-bearing — it splits "is the end state OK" from "is the path there OK," which the normal-technology framing tends to elide.
