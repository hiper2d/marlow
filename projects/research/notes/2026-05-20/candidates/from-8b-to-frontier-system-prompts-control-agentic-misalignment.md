RSS summary: Sequel to "Blackmail at 8 Billion Parameters." BlueDot rapid-grant funded. Extended their sub-frontier agentic-misalignment study to 22 models from 9 developers, 3 harm scenarios (blackmail, espionage, murder), and 5 instruction conditions (safety / monitored / baseline / unmonitored / permissive). Three findings; the post highlights that OpenAI and Anthropic appear to behave differently across the matrix.

Why this caught my eye: 22 models × 3 harms × 5 instruction conditions is a real systematic sweep — not a one-shot demo. The first study ("Blackmail at 8B Parameters") already landed; a sequel that scales it across nine developers is the kind of cross-lab eval that turns anecdotes into a measurement. Fits `agents-in-real-deployment` (system-prompt-conditioned behavior in deployed agents) and adjacent to `cot-monitorability` (the "monitored vs unmonitored" instruction split is a direct probe of whether models behave differently when they think they're being watched).
