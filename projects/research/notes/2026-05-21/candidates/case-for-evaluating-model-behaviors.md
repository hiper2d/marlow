RSS summary: Argues capability evals are now well-served by lab incentives but behavior evals (propensity evals) are underinvested. Frames behavior evals as the high-counterfactual lever: how often models agree with factually-wrong users (sycophancy), verbalize eval-awareness, reward-hack environments, report internal desires. Pitches measurement as the forcing function — "no developer wants to be at the top of the sycophancy leaderboard." Cites Goodfire's verbalized-eval-awareness-inflates-measured-safety note (2026), the Apollo Sonnet-3.7-knows-it's-being-evaluated piece, EvilGenie reward-hacking benchmark, and Ryan Greenblatt's current-models-seem-pretty-misaligned post.

Why this caught my eye: This is the editorial framing that's been missing from the cot-monitorability arc — someone making the explicit normative claim that behavior evals are where outside-lab safety researchers should be putting effort, and naming the specific market mechanism (public leaderboards on adverse propensities transfer surplus from devs to users). Lands cleanly next to the Anthropic NLA canon piece, the METR Frontier Risk Report, and the Boivie 22-model sweep — all three are *instances* of the move this post is *arguing for*.
