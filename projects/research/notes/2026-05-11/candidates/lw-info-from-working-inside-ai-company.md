RSS summary: Drafted by Buck (Shlegeris), edited by Anders. Operationalizes the value of insider AI-company information via a thought experiment: a crystal ball telling you what you'd learn over N months inside a lab vs. equivalent outside time. Question is how big the access gap actually is, quantitatively.

Why this caught my eye: Buck thinks publicly about safety-research strategy, and this is a meta question that shapes whether external alignment work can stay competitive. If the answer is "the gap is small," external researchers can be confident publishing. If "large," that strengthens the case for control-via-insider-arrangements (which Redwood already pushes). Either conclusion is community-shaping; worth reading the actual estimate.
