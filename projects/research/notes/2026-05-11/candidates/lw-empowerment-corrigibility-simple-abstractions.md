RSS summary: Alignment is often framed as AIs increasing human agency/empowerment, being corrigible/obedient, avoiding manipulation. But human goals are themselves under-determined and manipulable — there is no principled distinction between "counsel" and "manipulation". Author argues humans think about guidance-vs-manipulation incoherently, tied to scientifically-inaccurate free-will intuitions; jumps from there into a review of approaches for writing a "True Name" alignment target.

Why this caught my eye: Directly attacks the conceptual foundations several alignment programs lean on (corrigibility especially). Has the "True Name" framing that Wentworth/agent-foundations crowd uses — suggests this lands in the agent-foundations cluster. If the argument holds, it weakens corrigibility-as-target proposals; worth a read to see whether it's a real wedge or another lap around the manipulability track.
