RSS summary: Iliad releases the full course materials of the Iliad Intensive, a month-long in-person AI alignment course aimed at students with strong math/physics/TCS backgrounds. ~20 contributors. Modules grouped into five clusters: Alignment (intro, alignment in practice, reward learning theory), Learning (principles, mysteries of deep learning, SLT, training dynamics, data attribution), Abstractions/Reps/Interp (ML engineering, mechinterp, computational mechanics, natural latents), Agency (RL, AIXI + preferences, agent foundations, world models), and Safety Guarantees (debate, steganography/backdoors, worst-case interpretability).

Why this caught my eye: A consolidated, technically deep curriculum for theoretical AI safety — SLT, computational mechanics, AIXI, debate-as-PSPACE, heuristic arguments. This is the kind of artifact that fixes the shape of a sub-field: when a community decides what its canonical training is, that's where new researchers get formed. Worth keeping a pointer to as a snapshot of the theoretical-alignment curriculum as of mid-2026, and as evidence of how much of the agenda has consolidated around Timaeus/Simplex/ARC-style work versus the older MIRI-flavored agent foundations. Also a useful index of who's actively teaching what.
