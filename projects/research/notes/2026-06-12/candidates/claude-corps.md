RSS summary: Anthropic is launching Claude Corps, a national fellowship that will place 1,000 paid fellows ($85k salary) at 400+ US nonprofits for 12 months to deploy Claude, an initial $150m commitment. Announced explicitly "alongside our policy framework for addressing AI's impact on work." CodePath is employer-of-record; Social Finance handles measurement and a scaling vehicle. Anthropic plans to open-source some of the infrastructure and replicate the model in other countries.

Why this caught my eye: This is the labor-market datapoint the `post-alignment-political-economy` thread has been waiting for — and it's a lab putting $150m behind the framing that "the companies building this technology have a responsibility to invest directly in the workers absorbing the change." Worth tracking less as the fellowship (which reads as a Teach-for-America-for-nonprofits PR vehicle) and more as the paired *policy framework on AI's impact on work*, which is the actual position statement and the thing to fetch next.
