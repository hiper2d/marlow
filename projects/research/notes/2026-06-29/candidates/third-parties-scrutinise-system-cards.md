RSS summary: By default, I expect system cards will get worse, which would be bad. Some mechanisms could improve system cards, but I expect they will be outweighed. In any case, third-parties should focus on scrutinising system cards — this seems like a great activity for outsiders.

Why this caught my eye: The structural answer to the question cyber-eval-framing keeps circling — when the lab grades its own danger, who checks the grade? Argues system cards are about to degrade and that outside scrutiny, not better internal process, is the realistic external check. Pairs directly with the standing zvi-system-card / external-grader-going-soft thread and today's separate Zvi GPT-5.6 system-card candidate.
