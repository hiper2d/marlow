RSS summary: Paper-explainer on FinAcumen ("Financial Multimodal Reasoning via Self-Evolving Experience Memory Harness"), Guo/Zhou et al., Beijing UPT + Queen Mary London. An 8B Qwen3 model improves on financial reasoning "without changing a single weight" — gains come from a self-evolving experience-memory scaffold, not training.

Why this caught my eye: Another frozen-weights, improvement-in-the-scaffold result — the same shape as SkillOpt (-17 pick) and the inverse of Continual Learning Bench (frontier agents fail stateful learning, ICL beats persistent memory). Feeds automated-ai-rd's "prosaic RSI lives in the scaffold, not the weights" leg and agents-in-real-deployment's stateful-learning question. Worth a body-read at curate to see if the self-evolving memory is real continual learning or just retrieval dressed up; the channel is a paper-explainer, so the primary is the arXiv paper.
