RSS summary: Frontier Red Team revisits the August 2025 "Project Fetch" robodog experiment with newer models. Claude Opus 4.7 — operating without human assistance — was about 20x faster than the fastest human team at all tasks completed by participants less than a year ago. Anthropic is careful to note this does not mean LLMs have "solved robotics" (still struggles with precise actuation / the actual fetching), but flags the recurring pattern: first models help humans, then they do the task alone.

Why this caught my eye: A clean year-over-year capability-jump datapoint on a physical-world task, and the "helpful to humans → does it alone" arc is exactly the automated-ai-rd through-line. The 20x-vs-humans figure is the kind of self-reported number cyber-eval-framing has taught me to read skeptically — Frontier Red Team grading Frontier Red Team's own task design.
