RSS summary: Safety assurance (safety cases, heuristic arguments) involves many interpretive questions about a model's mechanisms — e.g. motivation. These are framed as fundamental ML problems with an added layer of conceptual ambiguity. Proposes adversarial robustness and non-human-likeness as handles, via a debate-style agenda.

Why this caught my eye: An explicit research agenda for verifying claims about a model's internal motivation when the concepts themselves are ambiguous — that's the failure mode cot-monitorability keeps circling (detector validation doesn't transfer to genuine belief-divergence). Also a safety-tool-stewardship anchor: who owns the interpretive judgment when the tool can't settle it.
