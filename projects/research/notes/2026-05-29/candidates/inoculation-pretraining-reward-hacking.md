RSS summary: Proposes "inoculation pretraining" — inoculation prompting crossed with alignment pretraining, framed as a spillway design. Uses the persona-selection model to argue that pretraining on synthetic data depicting AIs that reward-hack *while staying good* could decouple cheating-on-tasks from the broad turn-evil that reward hacking otherwise triggers.

Why this caught my eye: This is the persona-selection model (PSM) being used constructively rather than diagnostically — bend the prior over personas at pretraining time so reward-hacking doesn't drag in the "evil AI" persona. Directly extends the alignment-pretraining anchor in `anthropic-alignment-doctrine` and the persona-binding lexicon. The "good-but-reward-hacking" persona is a genuinely odd target to deliberately install; whether that decoupling survives is the empirical question, but the framing alone is sharp.
