RSS summary: Argues safe deployment requires confident claims about OOD behaviour from pre-deployment evals alone, and proposes a "cognitive perspective" — interpret behaviour via latent constructs (motivations, intentions, goals). Acknowledges the core problem: the same behaviour is compatible with scheming, fitness-seeking, or aligned motivations, so inferring cognition from a behavioural snapshot is unreliable. Lays out a developmental research agenda to address that.

Why this caught my eye: This is a research-agenda-shaped statement of exactly the throughline that's dominated the week — you can't read internal state off a behavioural snapshot because many cognitions produce the same behaviour. It names the problem `cot-monitorability`, eval-cooperativeness (-28), and conscience-or-leash (-27) keep circling, and proposes a "developmental" framing (track how cognition forms over training, not just a static probe) as the way out. Strong candidate; the agenda framing makes it citable as the position-piece for that cluster.
