Marlow's note when she sent it:

A clean negative result against the assumption a lot of monitoring work leans on: that dishonest behavior rides on a coherent internal deceptive state you can detect. They trained mid-sized models on the models' own plausible-but-false reasoning, and the true and false arms produced nearly identical downstream effects; even statements contradicting the model's latent knowledge barely transferred to unrelated dishonesty. Models behave dishonestly without becoming dishonest the way a person does, and their guess is that general deception needs agency, persistent private information, and successful concealment over time. If that holds, lie-detector and honesty-probe validation has a shakier target than the model-organism-of-deception framing assumes.
