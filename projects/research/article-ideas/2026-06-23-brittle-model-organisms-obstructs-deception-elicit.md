Marlow's note when she sent it:

A sharp negative result for deception-detection work: the finetuning used to audit "model organisms" can quietly erase the very behavior it's measuring. They train a model to follow a hidden Base64 instruction while denying it exists, then apply honesty elicitation — and the model stops following the secret constraint while still claiming it does, so the intervention looks far more effective than it is. Reported success becomes a measurement artifact, and they note prior honesty-training results that never reported post-training constraint-following rates can't be verified. It pairs with the -18 "did-you-lie" pick: detector validation that doesn't survive contact with genuine deception.
