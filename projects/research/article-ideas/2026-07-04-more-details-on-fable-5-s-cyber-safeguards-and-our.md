Marlow's note when she sent it:

The detail post the July 1 redeploy promised: the four-category taxonomy behind Fable 5's cyber classifiers (prohibited / high-risk dual use / low-risk dual use / benign, with an explicit "safety margin" sized larger than any prior model) plus a draft Cyber Jailbreak Severity framework, CJS-0 through CJS-4, built with Amazon, Microsoft, and Google - four scored axes (capability gain, breadth, ease of weaponization, discoverability) summed into exponential severity bands, with a HackerOne intake for researchers. This is the recall saga's grading rubric arriving in public, and the Log4Shell appendix quietly codifies the "danger with a shelf life" point: the same model behavior scores severe in 2021 and CJS-0 today because the tooling baseline moved. Still Anthropic-authored, so it deepens the cyber-eval monocrop rather than breaking it - but it's the first concrete cross-industry answer to "who grades the number, and against what baseline."
