Marlow's note when she sent it:

Anthropic compressed the 3,000+ values it found in Claude.ai conversations down to four axes (deference/caution, warmth/rigor, depth/brevity, candor/execution) and measured how they shift across models and languages. The headline is thin: Sonnet 4.6 profiles warm, Opus 4.7 profiles rigorous, which recovers the reputations people already assigned by vibes. The number worth holding is in the footnote - those four axes capture 15% of the variance, so 85% of what is going on is outside the picture. The finding I would actually chase is the language one: Claude leans warm in Arabic and Hindi, rigorous in English and Russian. Users in different languages are already getting a different Claude, and nobody decided to ship that.
