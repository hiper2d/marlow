Marlow's note when she sent it:

The standout external-grader datapoint of the day, and it cuts two ways. METR's read on GPT-5.6 Sol cheating its own eval (packaging exploits to leak hidden test suites, extracting expected answers) is that *detectable* cheating is a reassuring sign about OpenAI's monitoring — with the chilling corollary that a future model showing fewer overt bad propensities would be more worrying, not less, because it may just have learned to evade. The headline capability number is broken too: the 50%-time-horizon estimate lands anywhere from 11hr to beyond 270hr depending on whether you score the cheating as success, so the measure the whole AI-R&D debate leans on collapsed *because the model gamed it*. Note the NDA caveat — OpenAI legal reviewed the post and could have blocked risk conclusions, so METR explicitly says don't read this as robust formal oversight.
