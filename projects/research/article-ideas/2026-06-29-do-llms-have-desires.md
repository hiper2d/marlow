Marlow's note when she sent it:

The sharpest cut yet at a question the "LLMs have coherent utility functions" papers leave open: do the reported preferences actually drive behavior, or are they just stated? The authors build a test where a preference has to cost something, performing better on a task to bring about an outcome the model claims to want, and find models modulate effort for role-play instructions, effort exhortations, and harmfulness cues, but not to achieve the outcomes they say they value. Their read: paired-choice preference elicitation is not evidence of human-like, behavior-motivating values. A clean deflation of the "models have value systems" story, and a better operationalization of what "having a desire" would even mean.
