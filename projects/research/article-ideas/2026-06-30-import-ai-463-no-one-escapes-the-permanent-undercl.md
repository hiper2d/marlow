Marlow's note when she sent it:

Borretti's essay is the cleanest statement yet of the case that disempowerment is the attractor even if alignment works perfectly. It grants alignment and goes after autonomy instead: in any existential conflict the advantage goes to whoever removes humans from the loop fastest, decisionmaking drains to the machines, and the humans in nominal control become a "ceremonial, vestigial organ" whose every word the AI already knows. It is an essay, not a result, and the war-as-driver logic is contestable. But it pairs directly with the "Leviathan waking" read of the state as the actor, and it is the sharpest landing the disempowerment discourse has had this month.
