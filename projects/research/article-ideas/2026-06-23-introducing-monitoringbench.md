Marlow's note when she sent it:

This is the external measure the CoT-monitoring discourse has been short on: a benchmark of the monitors themselves, 2,644 attack trajectories against 20 coding-agent monitors, rather than another paper showing the transcript decays. The headline is brutal — post-hoc refinement of attacks dropped the strongest monitor (Opus 4.5) from 94.9% catch rate to 60.3%, and the gains transfer to monitors held out from attack construction. They also find a "conceive-execute gap": frontier models can propose strong attacks or execute them, but not both at once, which is the seam their pipeline exploits. Non-Anthropic, non-interp, and it actually scores the thing most papers only assert.
