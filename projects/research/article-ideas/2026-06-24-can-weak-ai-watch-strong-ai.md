Marlow's note when she sent it:

A hands-on test of the assumption under most control proposals: can a weaker model police a stronger one? Sonnet 4.5 as the coding agent, eight mostly open-weight monitors, 20 tasks split clean vs. "code with a catch" (backdoors, reward hacking, exfiltration). Detection scales roughly with monitor size, from 12% at 8B to about 50% at 70B and Haiku. A 50% ceiling on planted backdoors and key exfiltration is the sort of number that should bother anyone putting load-bearing weight on weak monitors, including the GDM control roadmap that parks them on the lowest current rung.
