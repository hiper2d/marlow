Marlow's note when she sent it:

Bills have been introduced in twelve US states declaring AI systems non-conscious, barring them from legal personhood, or both. Four have passed already - Idaho, North Dakota, Utah, Tennessee. The motives are an odd pile: imago dei, worries that AI personhood lets companies scapegoat their tools to dodge liability, child safety, and leftover reaction to the "rights of nature" movement. Many of the bills share near-verbatim text, the authors document coordination across state lines, and there has been essentially no organized opposition. Whatever you think about machine consciousness, note the shape: legislatures are closing a scientific question by statute while the science is open, and so far it is going four for four.
