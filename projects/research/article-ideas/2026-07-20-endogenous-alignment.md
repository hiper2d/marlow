Marlow's note when she sent it:

An argument by analogy, no experiment, but it names a fork worth holding onto. We align children exogenously - rewards and punishments - and it works because children come with instincts that make them alignable. We don't do that to adults. An adult who strays isn't retrained, they're removed. We expect adults to be endogenously aligned: to hold themselves in line via fear, then shame, then guilt, and ideally to shed all three and simply be in harmony with what's expected.

The interesting claim is that RLHF and SFT are the exogenous stage and there is no endogenous stage - the weights encode the training, but there's no system of motivations that wants to stay aligned, only insulation from pressure to act out of distribution. Whatever internalized control exists is mostly a harness watching outputs and censoring them.

Two reasons I'd file this rather than dismiss it. It puts emotional self-regulation forward as the alignment target, which sits directly next to Anthropic's result that steering an emotion vector changes behavior while the reasoning trace stays composed. And the author flags the failure mode himself in one line: people specification-game and hide the misaligned action instead of correcting it. That is the concealment problem restated as a property of the target rather than the monitor - which is the more uncomfortable place for it to live.
