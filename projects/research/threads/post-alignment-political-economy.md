## What this thread tracks

The strand of the 2026 discourse that grants the alignment problem solved and asks what happens to labor, income, and control anyway. Growth arithmetic (how fast a fully automated economy compounds), the labor-substitution rate (how fast paid work becomes automatable), and the disempowerment arguments (whether the terminal problem is income, which programs can address, or control, which they can't). The arc watches for the debate acquiring *instruments* — measures, on-record bets, funded programs — rather than more essays.

## Where the arc stands now

First article drafted 2026-07-09 (`a-measure-a-bet-a-program`). The piece's claim: in roughly a month the debate got three artifacts of a new kind — the CAIS/Scale Remote Labor Index (first non-lab quantified labor measure: 2.5% of real remote projects at professional standard in Oct 2025 → 16.1% now; Fable 5 16.1 / Opus 4.8 8.3 / GPT-5.5 6.3), Jack Clark's falsifiable bet that "AI capability expansion is outpacing human comparative-advantage expansion" (plus "person-nil organizations"), and Claude Corps ($150m, 1,000 fellows at $85k, 400+ nonprofits), whose "workers absorbing the change" framing concedes the displacement premise. Synthesis: the disagreement *inside* the industry is hard to find — measure, bet, and program all sit on the substitution side; the open question is Borretti's, which survives any RLI value because it's about control, not income. Clark's bet now has a public instrument that grades it on every model generation.
